# USP Generator

Transmutes features into promises. Produces the breakthrough market positioning that makes competitors irrelevant.

---

## LAYER 1: INVARIANTS

1. **USP differentiation happens at the PROMISE level, never features.** Competitors can replicate features effortlessly. Few can authentically deliver the same transformative promise.
2. **Every USP candidate must connect to a Level 1 desire** (from Reese's hierarchy). A USP disconnected from core human desire is positioning without power.
3. **The USP must be OWNABLE** — something competitors can't credibly claim even if they wanted to. If they could say the same thing, it's not differentiated.
4. **Market sophistication level determines which approach works.** A Level 1 market needs a new claim. A Level 5 market needs a new mechanism. Matching approach to sophistication is mandatory.
5. **Save all outputs to files.** Never deliver in chat only.

---

## PURPOSE

The USP is the Point B expressed as a deliverable promise.

Most businesses confuse features with benefits, and benefits with promises. Features describe the product. Benefits describe what it enables. Promises describe the transformation delivered. Only promises create true differentiation.

**The Three-Level Hierarchy:**

**Level 1: FEATURES** (What it IS)
- Tangible components and deliverables
- Modules, sessions, tools, elements
- What someone receives or accesses
- Example: "6 training videos"

**Level 2: BENEFITS** (What it ENABLES)
- Immediate advantages activated by the features
- Capabilities unlocked, possibilities created
- What becomes accessible and achievable
- Example: "Master material at your optimal pace"

**Level 3: PROMISES** (What it DELIVERS)
- The profound transformation or ultimate outcome
- The definitive result in their life/business
- The identity metamorphosis or status elevation
- Example: "Ascend to recognized authority status in your domain"

**Output use:** The USP drives: headline, lead, offer presentation, and competitive positioning across all marketing.

## STEP 1: FEATURE EXCAVATION & EXPANSION

Enumerate every single feature with forensic precision. Target 20-30 features total.

Categories to investigate:
- Visible deliverables (modules, sessions, videos, calls, templates)
- Hidden infrastructure features (support, community, access, updates)
- Proprietary methodology or process elements
- Unique delivery mechanisms
- Bundled value-add components
- Implementation architecture
- Access to people, networks, or resources
- Speed/timeline advantages

For each feature identified:
- Name it precisely
- Note if it's unique (no one else has it) or parity (standard in category)
- Identify the implicit promise it suggests

---

## STEP 2: FEATURE-TO-BENEFIT TRANSMUTATION

For each feature, articulate:
1. **Immediate benefit** — what it immediately enables
2. **Secondary benefit** — what that enablement makes possible
3. **Emotional benefit** — how it feels to have this advantage
4. **Social benefit** — how it changes how others perceive them
5. **Strategic benefit** — the competitive advantage it creates

Example chain:
- Feature: "Weekly live Q&A with Rich"
- Immediate benefit: "Get specific answers to your specific situation"
- Secondary benefit: "Avoid months of wrong-direction work"
- Emotional benefit: "Feel supported instead of figuring it out alone"
- Social benefit: "Access to top-level thinking that others pay $50K/year for"
- Strategic benefit: "Compress 2 years of learning into 90 days"

---

## STEP 3: BENEFIT-TO-PROMISE METAMORPHOSIS

For the apex benefits, excavate:
1. What fundamental transformation does this orchestrate?
2. What identity evolution does it catalyze?
3. What future state does it crystallize?
4. What endemic problem does it dissolve permanently?
5. What unprecedented possibility does it create?

Transform benefit statements into promise statements:
- NOT: "Learn marketing" (benefit)
- INTO: "Become the marketer your competitors can't keep up with" (promise)

Produce 10-15 distinct promise statements.

---

## STEP 4: COMPETITIVE DIFFERENTIATION ARCHITECTURE

For each promise candidate, scrutinize:

1. **Ownership test:** Can competitors credibly claim this promise? (If yes, eliminate or strengthen)
2. **Desire alignment:** Does this connect to a primary Level 1 desire? (Which one specifically)
3. **Believability test:** Is this promise credible from this platform/person? (What proof supports it)
4. **Category reframe test:** Does this define a new category or own an existing one?
5. **Market sophistication match:** Does this approach match the sophistication level of this market?

**Schwartz sophistication matching:**
- Level 1 (unaware): Lead with the result/promise directly — the big claim
- Level 2 (problem aware): Lead with the problem — they know something's wrong
- Level 3 (solution aware): Lead with the mechanism — show how/why this way works
- Level 4 (product aware): Lead with differentiation — why this over competitors
- Level 5 (most aware): Lead with the offer itself — they know what they want, just show the deal

---

## STEP 5: USP CANDIDATE SYNTHESIS

Produce 5-10 USP candidates across different formulas.

**USP Formula Types:**

**The Result USP:** "[Specific result] in [specific timeframe] or [guarantee]"
**The Mechanism USP:** "The only [category] that uses [specific mechanism] to [specific result]"
**The Identity USP:** "For [specific who] who [specific situation] and want [specific transformation]"
**The Anti-USP:** "The [category] for people who are tired of [what everyone else promises]"
**The Paradox USP:** "Achieve [desired result] without [unwanted cost/sacrifice]"
**The Category Creator:** "The first [category name you own] that [specific capability]"

For each USP candidate:

**USP CANDIDATE #[X]:**

*The Statement:* "[One-sentence USP]"

*The Formula Used:* [Which formula type]

*L1 Desire Connected:* [Which primary desire this channels]

*Ownership Analysis:* [Can competitors claim this? Why/why not?]

*Proof Requirements:* [What evidence validates this promise?]

*Market Sophistication Match:* [Which Schwartz level this targets and why it fits]

*Strengths:* [What makes this compelling]
*Weaknesses:* [What makes this risky or weak]
