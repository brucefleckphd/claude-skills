# Kern Close Frameworks

Complete framework set for the close section—the selling sequence using Kern's Ultimate Offer Structure with NLP patterns and conversion boosters.

## When to Use This Skill

- Creating the close section of your webinar
- Building the 12-step Ultimate Offer Structure
- Using NLP transitions from teaching to selling
- Adding boosters to increase conversion
- Creating desire-based CTAs and urgency

**Kern's Approach:** More about smooth NLP transitions and psychological boosters than aggressive selling. Tutorial-first, then seamless shift to offer.

## The Ultimate Offer Structure (Master Framework)

**The 12-step sequence (flexible—don't need all, don't need exact order):**

```
1. NLP CONFIRMATION      → Triple-fact agreement pattern
   ↓
2. REASON WHY INTRO      → "That's why I created [product]"
   ↓
3. BIG BENEFIT           → High-level outcomes list
   ↓
4. IF YOU WANT TO SERIES → Desire accumulation
   ↓
5. WALKTHROUGH           → A) Big picture B) Benefit breakdown
   ↓
6. PRICE                 → A) Value build B) Payment options
   ↓
7. BOOSTER #1            → Price drop for first X
   ↓
8. BOOSTER #2            → Guarantee (multiple slides)
   ↓
9. BOOSTER #3            → First bonus
   ↓
10. RECAP                → Sum all value
   ↓
11. BOOSTER #4           → Deadline/scarcity
   ↓
12. FINAL CLOSE          → Triple-fact NLP close
```

**Core Elements:** Product, price, risk reversal, bonus are non-negotiable. Boosters are flexible based on offer complexity.

**Critical Insight:** Each booster resets attention and gives new reason to buy. URL stays visible throughout so every new reason = conversion opportunity.

## The Three Critical Systems

### SYSTEM 1: The NLP Transition (Steps 1-2)

**K4. NLP Confirmation Opening**
- Use triple-fact pattern: "You're here with me [fact 1], listening to this webinar [fact 2], and you know selling with webinars is a good idea [what you want them to accept]"
- Creates agreement momentum before making offer
- Alternative NLP patterns: psychic, global, hype-free

**K5. Reason Why Intro**
- "That's why I've created [product name]"
- Establishes product as solution to what they just agreed they need
- Feels like natural next step, not abrupt shift

**Why it works:** They're nodding before you even mention the product. Smooth transition from teaching to selling.

---

### SYSTEM 2: The Desire Build (Steps 3-5)

**K6. Big Benefit Statement**
- "This program is specifically designed to help you [list 2-4 outcomes]"
- High-level benefits, not features
- Example: "Build a list, hold a webinar, make sales—all in 28 days"

**K7. If You Want To Series**
- "If you want to build authority... if you want loyal following... if you want highly responsive list... then this is specifically for YOU"
- Each statement gets own slide
- Accumulation creates "yes, yes, yes—this is for me!" momentum

**K8-K9. Complete Walkthrough**
- K8: Show structure (modules, phases, components)
- K9: Detail each component with "benefit so you can" statements
- Minimum one slide per component, more for complex components

**Why it works:** Progressively building desire while showing exactly what they get. By time you hit price, they already want it.

---

### SYSTEM 3: The Booster System (Steps 7-12)

**The Booster Concept:** Each booster resets attention and adds new reason to buy. Desire builds continuously while URL stays visible.

**K12. First Booster - Price Drop**
- "But it gets better. First 10 to order can make three payments of $300 [instead of $500]"
- Creates urgency (limited spots) and scarcity (first movers)
- Discount should be meaningful (20-40% off)

**K13-K14. Second Booster - Guarantee**
- Frame as another reason: "But it still gets better. You're protected by best guarantee ever"
- Break guarantee into multiple slides (one point per slide)
- Spell out: how long to try, how long to implement, how to get refund

**K15-K17. Third Booster - Bonuses**
- "If guarantee wasn't enough and great price wasn't enough, I want to do one better"
- Sell bonus with 'benefit so you can' statements (multiple slides)
- Can add multiple bonuses—each is conversion opportunity
- Psychological insight: Talking about bonuses doesn't feel like pitching because you're giving free stuff

**K18. The Recap**
- "Let me wrap it all up for you. This has been a lot"
- List: main product + all bonuses + discount + guarantee
- Creates "wow, all that?" moment

**K19. Fourth Booster - Deadline**
- Acknowledge offer is great, create urgency
- "I plan to raise price this week if there's even availability by end of week"
- Deadline must be REAL and legitimate

**K20. Final Close**
- Return to triple-fact NLP
- "You've watched this entire presentation, you want webinars that sell, you're still here so you know this is excellent value"
- "Therefore, go ahead, take advantage of this incredible discount, amazing bonuses, can't-lose guarantee now"

**Why it works:** Constantly giving new reasons to buy without feeling pushy. Each booster is value-add, not pressure.

## The Carltonian Formula Alternative

**For simpler offers (liquidator, low-ticket), use K2/K28:**

**Here's What I Got** → Describe product/offer clearly

**Here's What It'll Do For You** → Benefits/outcomes connected to desires

**Here's What I Want You To Do Next** → Clear single CTA

**Transition into it:**
"What I just gave you is an overview of the exact same system I used to [result]... and now that you know WHAT to do, I'm going to show you exactly HOW to do it."

This only works if you actually taught the WHAT first. Tutorial → transition → offer HOW.

## The Language Patterns

### K26-K27. "So You Can" Formula

Every component, feature, or benefit should use:

**"You get X SO YOU CAN Y"**

- X = the component/feature
- Y = the benefit connected to their desires

Example: "In module one, you learn five conversation starters SO YOU CAN always be confident approaching beautiful women without feeling weird."

**Test:** Cover X and read just Y—does it sound valuable? If yes, X is the way to get Y.

### K22. Desire-Based Button Copy

Instead of "Buy Now" or "Add to Cart":

**"Yes, I want to sell on webinars"**

Psychological difference:
- "Buy Now" = complying with your demand
- "Yes, I want X" = expressing their desire

They're saying yes to what THEY want, not submitting to what YOU want. Makes clicking feel like taking control, not being sold to.

## The CTA System

### K21. URL Omnipresence

From Step 7 (first booster) onward:
- Put URL at bottom of EVERY slide
- Say URL out loud frequently (every 2-3 slides minimum)
- Tell them exactly what happens: "Go to [URL], choose payment plan, immediately get login info, then weekly lessons"
- URL must be simple and memorable

### K23. Timed Buy Button (for VSL)

**When to show button:** Right when you mention the price

- Not before (seems pushy)
- Not too late (they may have left)
- Appears within 5 seconds after price mentioned
- Remains visible rest of video

### K25. Extension Story

After making offer, tell story about using your product to get specific benefit:
- Keeps button visible longer
- Continues building desire
- 2-3 minutes
- Adds new proof/angle not covered before
- Only use if offer is under 2 minutes (too quick)

## Kern's Close Principles

1. **Tutorial positioning** - Position as teaching, not pitching, until the transition
2. **NLP transitions** - Use triple-fact and agreement patterns to shift smoothly
3. **Booster philosophy** - Each booster resets attention and adds value
4. **Bonuses don't count** - Talking about free bonuses doesn't feel like selling
5. **URL omnipresence** - Every slide with URL = conversion opportunity
6. **Desire-based CTAs** - They say yes to their desire, not your demand
7. **Legitimate urgency** - Deadline must be real and logical

## Common Mistakes

**Mistake #1:** Abrupt shift from teaching to selling
- Violates NLP transition approach
- Use K4-K5 to create smooth bridge

**Mistake #2:** Generic button copy
- Violates desire-based CTA principle
- Use K22 to make button about their want

**Mistake #3:** Fake deadlines
- Violates legitimate urgency
- Use K24 only with real scarcity/deadlines

**Mistake #4:** Too few boosters
- Misses conversion opportunities
- Use K12-K20 to keep building value

**Mistake #5:** Hiding URL until end
- Violates omnipresence principle
- Use K21 to show URL from first booster on

## Validation Questions

**Before finalizing close:**
- Does this use NLP transition? (K4-K5)
- Am I using 'so you can' for every component? (K26-K27)
- Do I have multiple boosters? (K12-K20)
- Is URL visible from first booster through end? (K21)
- Is my button copy desire-based? (K22)
- Are my deadlines legitimate? (K24)
- Did I recap all value before final close? (K18)
