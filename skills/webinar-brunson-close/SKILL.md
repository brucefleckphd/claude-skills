## The Stack System (Master Framework)

**The fundamental methodology that made Russell Brunson over $10 million:**

### How It Works

```
1. Present Component 1 (core offer element)
   ↓
2. Present Component 2 + RESTACK (recap 1 & 2 with values)
   ↓
3. Present Component 3 + RESTACK (recap 1-3 with cumulative value)
   ↓
4. Add Bonus 1 + RESTACK (recap everything with total value)
   ↓
5. Add Bonus 2 + RESTACK (recap full stack with new total)
   ↓
6. Continue adding + restacking
   ↓
7. By the end, you've recapped the full offer 6+ times
```

### Why It Works

**The Psychology:** The human mind only remembers the last thing shown. If your last thing is Bonus #10 and it doesn't justify the price, they won't buy. The Stack solves this by making them remember EVERYTHING.

**The Validation:**
- Armand Morin: 30-50% of 1,000 people = ~$1M in sales
- Russell's first attempt: #1 salesperson, ~$100K
- Russell's career: $10M+ directly traceable to The Stack
- Natasha: $50K from 30 people at live event
- Russell's deviation: Thought he was "too smart," bombed completely

### How to Know You're Doing It Right

**The Embarrassment Test:** You should feel embarrassed by how repetitive it is. People should be laughing because it's getting ridiculous. If you're comfortable with the level of repetition, you're not doing enough restacking.

**Source quote:** "By the time you've done the stack or six times you're gonna be so embarrassed and I remember first time I did this at a seminar people were laughing and and it got kind of like ridiculous but guess what everybody knew exactly what the offer was and they bought like crazy."

## The Price Psychology System

### Phase 1: Build the High Price (Stack Value)

1. **Present core offer components** (3-5 items with individual values)
2. **Show cumulative value** after each addition ($8,000 → $11,998 → $14,995)
3. **Add bonuses one at a time** with stated values
4. **Restack after each addition** showing new cumulative total

### Phase 2: Justify the High Price (If-All Statements)

**CRITICAL:** Never discount before justifying!

```
"I could charge $11,998, but before I tell you what I'm actually charging,
let me ask you something...

If ALL this package did was [Benefit 1], would it be worth $11,998?
(Pause for mental agreement)

If ALL this package did was [Benefit 2], would it be worth $11,998?
(Pause for mental agreement)

If ALL this package did was [Benefit 3], would it be worth $11,998?
(Pause for mental agreement)"
```

These if-all statements solidify the high price as reasonable before you discount.

### Phase 3: Add Social Proof Price Layers

"We've had people pay us over a million dollars for something similar..."

This creates additional reference point that makes even the high stacked price seem like a bargain.

### Phase 4: Price Drop Sequence

"But don't worry, I'm not gonna charge you $11,998.
I'm not gonna charge you $5,000.
I'm not gonna charge you $2,000.
I'm gonna do it for just $1,000."

Each false high price creates additional contrast, making final price seem like extreme bargain.

### Phase 5: Continuous Call to Action

**After first price reveal:** CTA on EVERY subsequent slide. Every bonus, close, and element ends with "click the order form" or equivalent.

## The Sixteen Closes System

After initial price reveal and close, you have immediate buyers and fence-sitters. All remaining slides exist for ONE purpose: tip fence-sitters over the edge. Use multiple closes from different psychological angles.

### Money Objection Closes

**1. Disposable Income Reframe**
- Money they already spend (movies, dining) redirected to investment
- Money would be spent anyway - just invest it instead of consuming it

**2. Money Replenishes Close** ⭐ Tier 2
- Money is renewable (paycheck every two weeks)
- Time is not renewable (missed moments never return)
- Invest money to save time since money replenishes but time doesn't

**3. Asset vs Liability Framework**
- Poor buy liabilities (things that take money - cars, boats)
- Rich buy assets (things that generate money)
- Reference Kiyosaki's Rich Dad Poor Dad

**4. Money is Good (Tool for Exchange)**
- Remove emotional baggage around spending
- Money not evil, just neutral tool
- Like trading money for food at grocery store

**5. Money or Excuses Dichotomy** ⭐ Tier 2
- Binary choice: money OR excuses, not both
- Either sign up and make money, or come up with excuses
- Can't have both

**6. Money Has No Emotions**
- Address love-hate relationship with money
- Remove all emotion from it
- Just rational tool for exchange

**7. Investment vs Consumption Reframe**
- Consumption: money in, experience fades, money gone
- Investment: money in, turns into more money
- Your offer is investment that generates returns

### Time/Action Objection Closes

**8. Break Old Habits Close** ⭐ Tier 2
- People get excited but do nothing next day
- Old habits hard to break
- Only way to break habit is invest NOW
- Otherwise slide back to exact same routine

**9. Habit Breaking Requires Investment**
- Acknowledge implementation gap
- Investment itself is pattern interrupt
- Without investment, everything learned becomes useless

**10. Information Alone Is Useless**
- You've shared valuable information
- But information alone won't save them
- Need systems, action steps, coaching, accountability

### Value/Price Justification Closes

**11. Your Two Choices (High Price Justification)**
- Could charge $97, sell thousands, but can't serve anyone well
- OR charge $2,000, provide personal attention and better service
- High price is benefit because means more attention for them

**12. Quality Service Justifies Premium Price**
- Low price = many customers = no personal attention
- High price = fewer customers = more time devoted to each
- Most prefer more attention, which justifies higher price

**13. Limited Capacity Creates Value Perception**
- Explicitly limiting customers increases perceived value
- Signals higher service quality and personal attention
- People want to be in smaller, well-served group

### Identity/Social Pressure Closes

**14. Us vs Them (Tale of Two People)** ⭐ Tier 2
- Two types: timid/nervous who think it's cool but never act (don't want them)
- And action-takers who step up and produce results (our people)
- Force self-identification: which group are you in?

**15. Action-Taker Segmentation**
- Describe failures (never take action) vs successful people (you want)
- Make clear you only want action-takers
- Force audience to prove they're action-takers by buying

**16. Reluctant Hero Close** ⭐ Tier 2
- "I'm nothing special" - struggled with same things
- "I can't do X, I can't do Y, spent 10 years figuring this out"
- "You can do it the hard way too, or just plug in what I discovered"
- Removes "you're special, I'm not" objection

### Future Pacing Closes

**17. Their Two Choices (Future Pacing)**
- Choice 1: invest now, have system running and making money in 2-3 weeks
- Choice 2: end webinar, go to bed, wake up tomorrow doing exact same thing
- Make choice 2 sound miserable, choice 1 sound exciting

**18. Now and Later Close**
- NOW: current struggles, frustrations, things they don't know
- LATER: after investment, those problems magically disappear
- Paint stark contrast to make transformation tangible

### Process/Friction Reduction Closes

**19. Handhold Close (Process Walkthrough)**
- Walk through entire sign-up and onboarding process visually
- Show order form, where they enter info, what page appears after purchase
- Show how to log into members area, where to submit questions
- Remove intimidation by making process familiar before they start

**20. Process Transparency**
- Show exactly what happens after purchase
- Make unknown process known
- Removes major friction point for buyers intimidated by uncertainty

### Emotional Release Closes

**21. Say Goodbye Close**
- Have audience literally say goodbye to things they hate
- "Say goodbye to phone phobia forever"
- "Say goodbye to that fear"
- Sprinkle throughout stack when addressing specific pain points
- Verbalization creates psychological release

**22. Fear Dismissal Through Verbalization**
- When revealing bonus/feature that eliminates pain point
- Pause and have them verbally dismiss it: "Say goodbye to [specific fear] forever"
- Creates emotional release and closure

### Value Stacking Closes

**23. If You Only Got Close**
- After each stack element: "If you only got this piece alone, it would still be worth the investment..."
- Then reveal next item: "...but I'm also giving you..."
- Repeat throughout stack to continually re-justify price

**24. Continuous Value Re-Justification** ⭐ Tier 2
- After each element: "If you only got THIS alone, still worth it"
- Then reveal there's MORE
- Continuously re-justifies price while building anticipation

### Qualification/Lead Capture Closes

**25. Secure Voicemail Question Line**
- Offer voicemail line for remaining questions
- BUT require full contact details + credit card info (promise not to charge)
- Qualifies serious buyers vs tire-kickers
- Captures lead info for personal follow-up
- 25% sales increase from this one technique alone

**26. Voicemail Qualification Strategy** ⭐ Tier 2
- Set up using Google Voice number
- Gets full contact details and card info
- Allows personal follow-up to close
- Dramatically increases conversion on fence-sitters

### Final Push Close

**27. Your Close Close (Final CTA Push)**
- At very end, explicitly state webinar is over
- Direct them to take action RIGHT NOW
- Keep pushing specific URL or button to click over and over
- "Webinar's over, go sign up right now, click the button below, go to www.URL.com/go"

## The Selection Principle

**You don't have to use all closes every time.**

Pick the ones that:
1. Make the most sense for your offer
2. You genuinely enjoy delivering
3. Tie to your personality and feel authentic

Using too many can feel cheesy. Using the wrong ones for your personality will sound inauthentic.

**Russell's approach:** Sprinkle 6-10 closes throughout the stack process, picking ones that make the most sense and feel natural to deliver.

## Core Offer Structure

### The Core Training Program (3-5 Components)

**The Module-as-Day Mental Model:** Imagine someone hired you personally and came to your office for a week of training. What would you teach them each day?

- Day 1 = Module 1 (first step)
- Day 2 = Module 2 (second step)
- Day 3 = Module 3 (third step)
- Continue through 6-8 days

By end of week, they know your complete system.

**Sweet spot:** 6-8 modules
- Less than 6: feels incomplete
- More than 8: feels overwhelming

### The Irresistible Bonuses (2-4 High-Value Additions)

Core program = foundation. Bonuses = what makes it irresistible, slam-dunk, can't-say-no offer.

After defining core, brainstorm: **What additional elements would make this so good they HAVE to buy?**

**Bonus Selection Criteria:**
- Valuable enough to be standalone products
- Remove specific objections or add specific value
- Make core program easier/faster/better to implement
- Have credible dollar values attached

## The Complete Stack Presentation Flow

### Step 1: Present Core Offer (5-10 minutes)

```
Component 1: [Name] - [$X value]
(Describe benefit, future pace, show proof)

Component 2: [Name] - [$Y value]
RESTACK: "So you're getting [Component 1] worth $X,
plus [Component 2] worth $Y, total value $[X+Y]"

Component 3: [Name] - [$Z value]
RESTACK: "So you're getting [all three with cumulative value]"

Continue through 3-5 core components
```

### Step 2: Add Bonuses with Testimonials (15-20 minutes)

```
Bonus 1: [Name] - [$A value]
(Present bonus with excitement)
[Insert testimonial proving this bonus works]
RESTACK: "So now you're getting [full stack] worth $[Total]"
[Mini-close: "If you only got this alone, still worth it..."]

Bonus 2: [Name] - [$B value]
(Present bonus)
[Insert testimonial]
RESTACK: "So now you're getting [full stack] worth $[New Total]"
[Mini-close]

Continue through 2-4 bonuses
Each time: Bonus → Testimonial → Restack → Mini-close
```

### Step 3: Show Final Total High Price

"So the total value of this complete package is $11,998."

(Do NOT discount yet!)

### Step 4: Justify High Price (If-All Statements)

"Now, I could charge you $11,998, and honestly, it would be worth it. But before I tell you what I'm actually going to charge, let me ask you something...

If ALL this package did was [Benefit 1 - e.g., 'make your business profitable'], would it be worth $11,998?

If ALL this package did was [Benefit 2 - e.g., 'get you out of phone phobia forever'], would it be worth $11,998?

If ALL this package did was [Benefit 3 - e.g., 'give you your life back'], would it be worth $11,998?"

### Step 5: Add Social Proof Price

"We've had people pay us over a million dollars for something similar. Literally. And you're getting it for a fraction of that."

### Step 6: Price Drop Sequence

"But don't worry, I'm not gonna charge you $11,998.

I'm not gonna charge you $5,000.

I'm not gonna charge you $2,000.

I'm gonna do it for just $1,000."

### Step 7: First Call to Action

"So click the button below right now and lock in your spot at www.[URL].com/go"

### Step 8: Deploy Multiple Closes (20-30 minutes)

From here, EVERY slide has a CTA. Between CTAs, deploy 6-16 different closes:

```
[Close 1: Money Replenishes]
→ CTA: "Click button below"

[Close 2: Break Old Habits]
→ CTA: "Go to [URL]"

[Close 3: Us vs Them]
→ CTA: "Sign up now"

[Close 4: Reluctant Hero]
→ CTA: "Click the link"

[Close 5: Their Two Choices]
→ CTA: "Invest right now"

[Continue with 5-10 more closes, each with CTA]
```

### Step 9: Handhold Close

"Let me show you exactly what happens when you click that button..."

(Walk through order form, payment page, confirmation page, members area login, where to ask questions)

### Step 10: Final CTA Push

"Okay, the webinar's over. Go sign up right now. Click the button below. Go to www.[URL].com/go. Do it right now while you're excited and motivated. Don't wait. Go do it right now."

### Optional Step 11: Voicemail Question Line

"If you still have one question preventing you from moving forward, call our secure voicemail line at [number]. Leave your full name, address, phone number, and credit card number with expiration. Don't worry, I'm not going to charge you. I just want to make sure you're serious. I'll personally call you back and answer your question."

## Common Mistakes

**Mistake #1: Not restacking enough**
- Violates The Stack methodology
- Should feel embarrassingly repetitive
- If comfortable, not doing enough

**Mistake #2: Discounting before justifying**
- Violates Value Justification Before Discount
- Destroys all credibility
- Anchor becomes worthless

**Mistake #3: Only closing once**
- Violates Sixteen Closes Principle
- Only immediately ready will buy
- Fence-sitters need multiple psychological angles

**Mistake #4: No CTAs after price reveal**
- Violates Continuous Call to Action Pattern
- Should have CTA on EVERY slide after price
- All remaining slides push to order

**Mistake #5: Thinking you're "too smart" for this**
- Violates The One-Time Deviation Failure lesson
- Russell bombed when he thought he had better idea
- Test beats taste, proven methodology beats ego
