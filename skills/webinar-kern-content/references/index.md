## Master Principles (Ace/King)

### C1. PAYOFF - Three Forms of Influence Hierarchy
**Tier:** 1 (Ace)
**Core Focus:** Demonstration > Testimonials > Claims
**When to Use:** Understanding why demonstration beats testimonials in webinar content
**Key Insight:** What you demonstrate has highest influence. If you fly in front of them, they believe. Payoff = demonstrate by helping.

### C2. It's About Them, Not You
**Tier:** 1 (King)
**Core Focus:** Talk more about THEM than yourself, their outcome than your stuff
**When to Use:** Creating any marketing content, diagnosing why content isn't performing
**Key Insight:** Universal principle. Video should be about them and their desired outcome. Count you/your vs I/we - ratio should be 3:1.

### C3. Tutorial Over Hype Principle
**Tier:** 1 (King)
**Core Focus:** Position as tutorial, actually teach 3 major things
**When to Use:** Deciding how much to teach in sales presentations
**Key Insight:** Feels like it sells better, leads to happier customers, attracts better buyers, easier on psyche, maintains goodwill.

### C4. Ladder of Desire Framework
**Tier:** 1 (King)
**Core Focus:** Do it FOR me > Do it WITH me > Give me the manual
**When to Use:** Positioning value and desirability of offer, especially high-ticket
**Key Insight:** Most people sell manual (least desirable). Sweet spot is "Do It WITH Me" or hybrid of levels 1 and 2.

## Core Teaching Frameworks (Queen/Jack)

### C5. Payoff Core Purpose
**Tier:** 2 (Queen)
**Core Focus:** Demonstrate you can help them by actually helping them
**When to Use:** Designing teaching section of webinar
**Key Insight:** Teach 3-4 core things they need. Actionable, not just theory. Bog-free: avoid boring details, focus on concepts.

### C6. X Steps to Y Formula
**Tier:** 3 (Jack)
**Core Focus:** "The 5 Pillars of [Result]" or "X Steps to Y"
**When to Use:** Structuring payoff teaching section headline
**Key Insight:** Time-tested formula used by all magazines. List all steps briefly, then teach each individually with separate slides.

### C7. Actionable vs Bog-Free Balance
**Tier:** 2 (Jack)
**Core Focus:** Actionable (they CAN do it) but not boring details
**When to Use:** Deciding level of detail for each teaching point
**Key Insight:** Teach fundamentals, patterns, examples. Don't walk through complex implementation step-by-step. Mention tools, move on.

### C8. Reason Why Advertising in Webinars
**Tier:** 2 (Jack)
**Core Focus:** Always explain WHY, not just what
**When to Use:** Explaining any concept or teaching point in payoff
**Key Insight:** Every 'what' should have a 'why' within 30 seconds. Use 'here's why,' 'because,' 'so you can,' 'the reason is.'

### C9. Proof Through Helping (Not Just Claims)
**Tier:** 1 (Jack)
**Core Focus:** Prove you can help BY ACTUALLY HELPING
**When to Use:** Building credibility, overcoming skepticism, demonstrating expertise
**Key Insight:** More powerful than credentials or testimonials. Teach valuable content in sales messages to prove capability through demonstration.

## Video & Attention Frameworks (10)

### C10. Kern's Three-Step Video Formula
**Tier:** 2 (10)
**Core Focus:** Pattern Interrupt → Payoff → CTA
**When to Use:** Creating any marketing video, structuring video content
**Key Insight:** First 9 seconds determines if rest gets watched. Payoff must be about THEM. Every video needs clear CTA.

### C11. The 9-Second Rule for Videos
**Tier:** 2 (10)
**Core Focus:** Video has 9 seconds to live
**When to Use:** Creating video openings, testing video performance
**Key Insight:** Must grab attention AND sell watching next segment. Track retention at 9-second mark. Goal: 70-80% still watching.

### C12. Questions Cannot Be Ignored (Brain Science)
**Tier:** 2 (10)
**Core Focus:** Brain must acknowledge every question
**When to Use:** Getting attention, controlling focus, starting any presentation
**Key Insight:** Makes questions most powerful attention control tool. You can refuse to answer but cannot ignore question.

### C13. Focus Control Through Questions
**Tier:** 2 (10)
**Core Focus:** What-if questions force mental pictures
**When to Use:** Directing attention, grabbing focus, controlling what prospects think about
**Key Insight:** Use result-oriented what-if questions. Series of questions forces series of mental pictures. Must be about THEM.

### C14. 'Imagine This Scenario' Pattern Interrupt
**Tier:** 3 (10)
**Core Focus:** Causes reflection, then questions take control
**When to Use:** Starting any presentation, redirecting attention, setting up what-if questions
**Key Insight:** Strangely, you DON'T describe scenario. Go straight into questions. Phrase puts them off-balance, questions direct focus.

## Story & Connection (10)

### C15. The Roadmap (Actual Content Preview)
**Tier:** 3 (10)
**Core Focus:** In story, briefly mention what you learned that led to success
**When to Use:** In BOND story section, after turning point, to tease payoff
**Key Insight:** Creates bridge from story to teaching. Don't teach yet - just preview. Good roadmap creates anticipation.

### C16. Feel-Felt-Found Through Story
**Tier:** 3 (10)
**Core Focus:** They FEEL skeptical → You FELT same → You FOUND solution
**When to Use:** Using story to create identification and overcome objections
**Key Insight:** Story shows your vulnerability and struggle. Creates identification without explicitly using formula.

### C17. Story as Metaphor for Point
**Tier:** 3 (10)
**Core Focus:** Stories make points more efficiently than flat statements
**When to Use:** Illustrating point or overcoming objection through narrative
**Key Insight:** When someone hears story, they place themselves in shoes of person it's about. Creates identification.

### C18. Feel-Felt-Found Formula
**Tier:** 2 (10)
**Core Focus:** I know how you feel → I felt same way → Until I found [solution]
**When to Use:** Addressing skepticism in copy, webinar intro, VSL
**Key Insight:** Validates skepticism rather than dismissing it. Creates kinship. Naturally transitions to teaching. Use after making bold claim.

## Slides & Structure (10)

### C19. Title Slide with Bullet Points Structure
**Tier:** 3 (10)
**Core Focus:** Title at top, 3-5 bullet points
**When to Use:** Creating slides for payoff teaching section
**Key Insight:** Kern's simple slide webinar: $140K from 1500 attendees ($100/attendee). No fancy graphics needed. Test simple first.

### C20. Concept and Supporting Bullets Pattern
**Tier:** 3 (10)
**Core Focus:** Concept title → 3-5 bullets → Example → Key takeaway
**When to Use:** Breaking down each pillar or step in payoff teaching
**Key Insight:** Each major point gets this treatment. Simple → Complex → Clear within 2-3 minutes per concept.

### C21. Big Statement Emphasis Slides
**Tier:** 3 (10)
**Core Focus:** Single powerful statement, no bullets
**When to Use:** After teaching complete concept, before moving to next
**Key Insight:** Resets attention between concepts. Use authoritative tonality. Don't overuse - 1-2 per major section max.

### C22. Boxes and Squares Visual Simplicity
**Tier:** 4 (10)
**Core Focus:** Simple boxes and arrows for process flow
**When to Use:** When you need to show process or sequence visually
**Key Insight:** Easy for brain to lock onto. Don't overcomplicate. Only diagram when clearer than words alone.

### C23. Situation-Solutions Worksheet Application
**Tier:** 3 (10)
**Core Focus:** Build payoff content from your Module 1 research
**When to Use:** Building content for payoff section from earlier research
**Key Insight:** Reference Bag of Tricks worksheet. Your pillars/steps address obstacles from research. Teach solutions to their situations.

## Supporting Tactical (10)

### C24. Avoid Bogging in Teaching
**Tier:** 2 (10)
**Core Focus:** Overview without excessive technicality
**When to Use:** Deciding what level of detail to include in teaching
**Key Insight:** Teach concepts, strategies, ideas - not deep technical implementation. The 'what' and 'why,' not complete 'how' detail.

### C25. Demonstration > Everything
**Tier:** 2 (10)
**Core Focus:** Show capability through action, not words
**When to Use:** Throughout payoff section when proving your expertise
**Key Insight:** Demonstration = teaching that gets results. If you claim you can fly: few believe. If 100 testify: some believe. If you FLY: most believe.
