---

## Purpose

Extracts a person's unique brand DNA from existing materials and creates 8 AI-optimized files that power all copywriting and webinar systems with THEIR voice, THEIR stories, THEIR credentials.

## Voice DNA Quality Standards

### MUST INCLUDE in 01-VOICE-AND-TONE.md:

1. **Real Voice Samples Section** (at TOP of file)
   - Actual Instagram captions (verbatim)
   - YouTube transcript excerpts (verbatim)
   - Real email/post examples

2. **Casual Writing Patterns**
   - Profanity level and examples
   - Emoji usage patterns
   - Word stretching ("mannyyy", "sooo")
   - Capitalization for emphasis
   - Self-deprecation patterns

3. **Speaking Patterns** (from video/audio)
   - Lowercase conversational energy vs. HIGH ENERGY
   - Filler phrases
   - Story structure
   - How they start/end

4. **Platform-Specific Calibration**
   - How voice shifts between Instagram, YouTube, LinkedIn, Email
   - Formality gradient

5. **Mission-to-Linguistic-Rules Translation** *(addition)*
   - Core values listed explicitly (e.g., "curious," "rigorous," "warm")
   - Each value translated into a concrete writing instruction (e.g., "curious → use inquisitive language and open-ended framing")
   - Ensures abstract identity is actionable for AI tools and human writers

6. **Audience Tonal Profiles** *(addition)*
   - 2–3 audience segments with psychographic depth: who they are, pain points, goals, online hangouts
   - Per-segment tonal guidelines showing how core voice adapts without losing fundamental identity
   - Example: "When writing for [segment], lean toward [tone adjustment] while maintaining [core trait]"

### Content Sample Quantity Standards *(addition)*

When curating exemplary content for voice training:
- **Prompt-only use**: 20–50 paragraphs/posts minimum
- **RAG or system-prompt grounding**: 30–75 pieces recommended
- **Fine-tuning a custom model**: 50–100 examples minimum; optimal results at 1,000+ samples
- **Quality criteria**: high-performing content (got shares/replies/conversions), customer communications (reveals empathetic voice), structured prompt-response pairs

### MUST NOT:
- Create voice guidelines from polished marketing copy alone
- Assume voice from website copy (often ghostwritten)
- Skip social media analysis
- Use only professional bio language

## Process

### Phase 1: Source Collection (LIVE FEEDS REQUIRED)

1. **Request source materials** from user:
   - Website URL
   - LinkedIn profile
   - Instagram handle
   - YouTube channel
   - Any existing bios, presentations, testimonials
   - Mission statement and core values (3–5) *(addition)*
   - Audience segments or personas if available *(addition)*

2. **Access live social media feeds**:
   - Open Instagram and scrape recent captions
   - Open YouTube and pull video transcripts
   - Review LinkedIn posts (not just profile)
   - Check other platforms mentioned

3. **Capture REAL voice samples**:
   - Copy actual posts verbatim
   - Note casual language patterns
   - Document emoji usage
   - Note profanity/casual language
   - Capture stretched words, ALL CAPS patterns

4. **Curate content training set** *(addition)*:
   - Collect 30–50+ pieces of best-performing content
   - Prioritize: posts with high engagement, emails that got replies, ads that converted
   - Include customer-facing communications if available (reveals empathetic register)
   - Flag the 5–10 pieces that most accurately represent the desired voice

### Phase 2: Voice Verification (GATE)

**STOP and verify you have:**
- [ ] Minimum 3 real social media posts captured
- [ ] At least 1 video transcript excerpt
- [ ] Documented: profanity level, emoji patterns, capitalization habits
- [ ] Identified difference between polished/casual voice
- [ ] Mission statement and core values collected *(addition)*
- [ ] At least 1 audience segment defined *(addition)*

**If missing**: Go back to Phase 1. Do not proceed.

### Phase 2.5: Mission & Values Translation *(addition)*

Before creating files, translate abstract values into concrete linguistic instructions:

1. List the person/brand's 3–5 core values explicitly
2. For each value, generate a specific writing rule:
   - "Curious" → "Use inquisitive language; open questions over declarative statements"
   - "Direct" → "Lead with the point; no throat-clearing preambles"
   - "Warm" → "Use 'we' and 'you' frequently; acknowledge emotions before advice"
3. Cross-check: do the real social media samples confirm these rules, or contradict them?
4. Document confirmed rules in 01-VOICE-AND-TONE.md under "Values-in-Voice" section

**This ensures the voice guide captures WHO they are, not just HOW they sound.**

### Phase 3: File Creation

Only after Phase 2 gate is passed:

1. Create 01-VOICE-AND-TONE.md with **Real Voice Samples section FIRST**
2. Add Values-in-Voice section (from Phase 2.5) and Audience Tonal Profiles section
3. Create remaining files from static sources
4. Cross-reference voice patterns across all files
5. Ensure CLAUDE.md index references the voice standards and includes AI strategy recommendation

### Phase 4: Quality Check & Human-in-the-Loop Revision *(updated)*

**Completeness checklist:**
- [ ] 01-VOICE-AND-TONE.md has verbatim social media quotes
- [ ] Casual patterns documented (not just professional voice)
- [ ] Platform-specific guidance included
- [ ] AI guidelines reflect REAL voice, not aspirational voice
- [ ] Values-in-Voice section present with concrete linguistic rules *(addition)*
- [ ] Audience tonal profiles included for at least 1 segment *(addition)*

**Human-in-the-Loop Revision Protocol** *(addition)*:

Apply the 80/20 rule to Voice DNA files:
- AI handles 80% of initial generation and pattern identification
- Human (user) dedicates 20% to strategic refinement: catching nuance AI missed, correcting over-generalizations, adding cultural context

Review cadence recommendation:
- **Week 1**: User reviews 01-VOICE-AND-TONE.md against 5 real posts — does it match?
- **Monthly**: Review AI-generated content using these files against the voice document; flag deviations
- **Quarterly**: Full tone audit — pull 20 pieces of AI-assisted content and score adherence

When the user identifies a mismatch, document the correction and update the relevant Voice DNA file so it functions as RLHF (reinforcement learning from human feedback) for future use.

## Phase 5: Governance & Drift Prevention *(addition)*

Once Voice DNA files are deployed, prevent tone drift with these practices:

**1. Audit Your Training Inputs for Bias**
- An LLM mirrors whatever bias exists in curated samples
- Review the content set for niche slang, exclusionary language, or outdated references
- Ensure samples are representative and inclusive of the voice at its best

**2. Use Factual Grounding for Claims**
- If the Voice DNA files will power content containing statistics, product specs, or verifiable claims, recommend a RAG (retrieval-augmented generation) setup
- Without RAG, AI may hallucinate facts while maintaining perfect tone — a legal and credibility risk

**3. Conduct Regular Tone Audits**
- "Voice creep" — gradual drift from original voice — happens silently
- Quarterly: pull a sample of AI-generated content and score it against 01-VOICE-AND-TONE.md
- If drift detected: trace back to prompt or training content; update files accordingly

**4. Data Privacy Guidelines**
- Be explicit with the user about what they're uploading to third-party AI tools
- Proprietary customer communications or confidential business content may violate agreements
- Recommend providers with strict data-use policies for sensitive material

## Recommended AI Strategy

Based on [client name]'s content volume and industry:

- **Prompt-only**: Best for low-volume use, easy to start, paste Voice DNA files as context
