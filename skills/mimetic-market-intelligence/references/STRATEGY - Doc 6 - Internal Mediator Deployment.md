## The Core Problem

Rich Schefren's social proof portfolio is structurally inverted. His marketing leads with external mediators -- Tony Robbins, Jay Abraham, Russell Brunson, Frank Kern, the $15 billion client results claim, the billion-dollar Agora testimonial. These create authority. They do not create desire.

In Girard's framework, the distinction is precise: an external mediator is someone the prospect admires but does not compete with. The prospect looks at Tony Robbins endorsing Rich and thinks, "Rich must be legitimate." But the prospect does not think, "I need to have what Tony Robbins has through Rich's program" -- because Tony Robbins is too far away. There is no rivalry. No jealousy. No urgency born from watching a peer pull ahead.

An internal mediator is someone close enough to the prospect that their success creates competitive tension. The prospect looks at a peer -- someone who was at their level, running a similar business, facing the same problems -- and sees that person operating at a visibly higher level after working with Rich. That proximity creates the specific psychological pressure Girard identified: "They were like me. They got THAT. I do not have THAT. This is intolerable."

Rich needs both types. But right now, the ratio is approximately 90/10 in favor of external mediation. It should be closer to 40/60 -- authority established by external mediators, desire driven by internal mediators.

This document provides the concrete plan for building, collecting, and deploying internal mediators across Rich's marketing.

## Section 1: The Internal Mediator Audit Framework

Every testimonial, case study, and social proof element in Rich's marketing should be scored on five dimensions. A score of 4-5 on three or more dimensions means the proof point functions as an internal mediator. Fewer than that, and it functions as credibility only.

### Dimension 1: Model Proximity (1-5)

How close is this person to the prospect's world?

- **1 — Distant celebrity.** Tony Robbins, Jay Abraham. The prospect has no contact with this person and never will. Pure authority signal.
- **2 — Industry legend.** Russell Brunson, Frank Kern. The prospect knows who they are but does not operate in their sphere. Credibility signal with mild aspirational pull.
- **3 — Successful peer.** A $2M-$10M business owner in a related space. The prospect might encounter them at an event or in a community. Beginning of mimetic tension.
- **4 — Direct peer.** A business owner at a similar revenue level, in a similar niche, facing similar problems. The prospect could easily imagine being this person -- or being compared to them by mutual contacts. Strong mimetic activation.
- **5 — Ambient rival.** Someone the prospect already knows, follows, or competes with. When this person visibly succeeds through Rich's program, the prospect feels it in their chest. Maximum mimetic pressure.

### Dimension 2: Achievement Relevance (1-5)

Did this person achieve something the prospect specifically wants?

- **1 — Vague endorsement.** "Rich is brilliant." No specific achievement referenced.
- **2 — General business success.** "My business grew." No specifics the prospect can compare against.
- **3 — Category-relevant achievement.** "I built an AI system for my business." Relevant but not specific enough to trigger comparison.
- **4 — Specific, measurable result.** "I grew revenue 40% in 3 months using what Rich taught." The prospect can immediately compare this to their own numbers.
- **5 — The exact result the prospect is trying to achieve.** "I went from doing everything myself to having AI handle 80% of my marketing production -- and my team of 4 now operates like a team of 20." This is the specific transformation the Force Multiplier buyer is purchasing.

### Dimension 3: Temporal Proximity (1-5)

How recent is the transformation?

- **1 — Historical.** "I worked with Rich in 2015." Too long ago -- the prospect wonders if it still applies.
- **2 — Pre-AI era.** "Rich helped me scale to $5M." Impressive but the prospect is buying AI transformation, not pre-AI strategy.
- **3 — Recent but unspecified.** "Rich's program changed my business." No time marker.
- **4 — Clearly recent.** "In the last 6 months, after going through Force Multiplier..." The prospect knows this is current.
- **5 — Ongoing/live.** "Right now, as I'm going through Connect the Dots, here's what's happening..." The transformation is in motion. Maximum urgency because the prospect feels they are falling behind in real time.

### Dimension 4: Specificity (1-5)

Are the results concrete enough to trigger direct comparison?

- **1 — Pure emotion.** "This was amazing!"
- **2 — Directional.** "My business improved significantly."
- **3 — Category-specific.** "My marketing got much better."
- **4 — Quantified.** "Revenue up 40%, client acquisition cost down 60%, I saved 15 hours per week."
- **5 — Quantified with context.** "I was running a 6-person marketing agency doing $1.2M. After Force Multiplier, we're doing $2.1M with the same team because AI handles the production workflow. My personal hours dropped from 55 to 35 per week." The prospect can locate themselves in this story -- same size business, same challenge, different result.

### Dimension 5: Visibility (1-5)

Will the prospect encounter this person in their professional world?

- **1 — Invisible.** Anonymous testimonial. No name, no business, no way to verify or encounter.
- **2 — Named but unfindable.** "John S., business owner." The prospect cannot locate this person.
- **3 — Named and findable.** Full name, business name, LinkedIn profile. The prospect could look them up but probably will not.
- **4 — Visible in shared spaces.** This person posts in communities, speaks at events, or runs a business the prospect follows. Encountering them is likely.
- **5 — Ambient presence.** This person is visibly a "Strategic Profits client" in the prospect's feed, community, or professional circle. Their success is a constant, low-grade reminder. This is the Two Comma Club effect -- you cannot escape the wall of winners.

### Scoring Application

When auditing existing proof:
- **Score 20-25:** This is a true internal mediator. Feature prominently and widely.
- **Score 15-19:** Strong proof point with internal mediator potential. May need repositioning (add specifics, increase visibility).
- **Score 10-14:** Functions primarily as credibility. Use for authority-building, not desire activation.
- **Score below 10:** Weak proof. The Tony Robbins quote is a 6 at best -- high authority, zero mimetic desire.

After ZenithMind: they have an AI copilot that reflects their patterns back to them, interrupts self-sabotage, and keeps them operating at their strategic best. The key detail: vulnerability. This mediator admits to the same internal struggles the prospect feels ashamed of.

**Flagship ($50K/year):**
The ideal mediator is someone the prospect genuinely envies -- a business owner operating at a visibly higher level, with access to everything Rich's operation builds, staying permanently ahead of the curve. The key detail: this mediator does not just have RESULTS. They have ACCESS. And the prospect wants that access with the same intensity they want results.

### Case Study Interview Structure

Standard testimonial interviews capture results. Mimetic mediator interviews capture IDENTITY TRANSFORMATION. The interview must answer:

**Before:**
- Who were you before this program? (Identity, not just situation)
- What were you struggling with that you had not told anyone about? (Vulnerability creates identification)
- What had you already tried that did not work? (Establishes proximity to the prospect's experience)

**The Moment:**
- What specifically happened during the program that shifted something? (The turning point must be concrete)
- What did you build, see, or understand that you could not before? (Specificity triggers comparison)
- Was there a moment of frustration or difficulty? What happened? (This is critical -- the struggle makes the transformation real, not magical)

**After:**
- Who are you now? How would you describe yourself differently? (Identity label)
- What specific, measurable changes have occurred? (Numbers for comparison)
- What can you DO now that you could not do before? (Capability shift)
- How do the people around you see you differently? (Social proof of transformation)

**The Ambient Question:**
- Would you be willing to be publicly associated with this program? To speak about it? To be visible as someone who went through this? (This question identifies candidates for the highest-impact deployment -- people who will become ambient mediators in the prospect's world)

## Section 4: The Deployment Map

### Landing Pages

**Current structure (typical):** Hero section with Rich's credibility, features/benefits, then testimonials at the bottom. This is the standard authority-first layout. It works for credibility but delays desire activation.

**Recommended structure:**
- Hero: The identity transformation promise (who you become, not what you get)
- Immediately below hero: 2-3 internal mediator stories -- peers who made this exact transformation. Short format: photo, name, business type, one-sentence before, one-sentence after, one specific number.
- Mid-page: Rich's credibility (external mediators -- the guru-to-the-gurus positioning). This validates the promise the internal mediators already activated.
- Features/curriculum: What the program contains
- Long-form case studies: 2-3 detailed before/during/after narratives (full identity transformation stories)
- Final CTA: Surrounded by additional internal mediator quotes

The ratio on the page should be approximately 60% internal mediators, 40% external mediators and credibility claims. Authority gets the prospect to trust. Peers get the prospect to ACT.

### Email Sequences

Internal mediators should appear strategically across the nurture sequence:

- **Email 2-3 (early nurture):** Introduce the first peer story. Keep it relatable and vulnerability-forward. "This is Sarah. She was exactly where you are."
- **Email 5-6 (mid-nurture):** Introduce a peer story with specific, quantified results. The prospect has been warmed up -- now hit them with comparison data.
- **Email 8-9 (pre-close):** Deploy the "ambient rival" -- a peer story featuring someone in the prospect's actual industry or niche. "Here's what Mark is doing in his marketing agency with this system."
- **Post-webinar follow-up:** The most intense internal mediator deployment. After the webinar has established Rich's authority, the follow-up emails should be almost entirely peer stories. Authority is established. Now it is time for desire.

### Webinars and Sales Presentations

**Current structure (typical for the market):** Presenter establishes authority, teaches content, makes offer, shows testimonials during the close.

**Recommended restructure:**
- Open with a brief internal mediator story before Rich even introduces himself. "Before I get started, let me tell you about someone who was on this webinar six months ago..." This primes desire before authority.
- Teach content with Rich's full authority (this is his strength -- keep it)
- During the offer section: LEAD with internal mediator stories, not features. "Here's what Force Multiplier includes" is less powerful than "Here's what happened to three people who went through Force Multiplier." Show the transformation, then reveal the mechanism.
- Close with 4-6 rapid-fire internal mediator quotes, each from a different type of business, creating the "wall of winners" effect. The prospect should see at least one person who looks like them.

### Social Media and Content

This is where internal mediators solve Rich's visibility problem without requiring Rich to become a personal-brand content machine:

- **Client spotlight content:** Weekly or bi-weekly posts featuring a client's transformation story. Rich introduces them, they tell their story. Rich does not need to be "on" -- the clients carry the content. This transfers visibility to the BUSINESS (Strategic Profits as the entity that produces these transformations) rather than to Rich as a personality.
- **"Built with Rich" showcase:** Monthly deep-dive content showing what a specific client built during Force Multiplier or Connect the Dots. This is not a testimonial -- it is a demonstration of capability that happens to feature a client.
- **Client co-creation content:** Bring clients onto a podcast, live stream, or interview series. Rich facilitates; the client speaks. This scales Rich's presence without scaling his personal effort, and every episode creates a new internal mediator.

### Community as Ambient Mediation

The most powerful deployment is structural, not content-based:

- **Make the community visible to prospects.** If ZenithPro or Force Multiplier alumni have a private community, give prospects LIMITED visibility into it -- a public feed, highlight reel, or "what happened in the community this week" digest. The prospect sees peers operating at a higher level and wants IN.
- **Create status markers within the community.** Levels, achievements, or recognition tiers that members display publicly (in their LinkedIn bios, email signatures, or social profiles). Every display is ambient internal mediation.
- **Encourage cross-pollination.** When a community member posts their success publicly, amplify it through Strategic Profits channels. The member gets recognition; Strategic Profits gets a new internal mediator deployed into the wild.

### The Distinctions Play as Internal Mediator Delivery Vehicle

Rich is developing a content initiative: release 10 mind-blowing AI distinctions publicly for free (no opt-in, no gate), seed them everywhere, encourage sharing -- then offer the remaining 90 distinctions for free WITH an opt-in. These are not generic tips. These are the same distinctions Rich used when advising Jay Abraham and Perry Marshall -- intellectual ammunition that demonstrably changes how smart operators think about AI.

From a mimetic standpoint, the Distinctions Play is a natural internal mediator delivery mechanism if the content is structured correctly:

- **Weave peer stories into the distinctions.** Each of the 10 free distinctions should include a brief narrative of a real person who applied it and what happened. "Distinction #4: [the distinction]. When Sarah K., a $2M consulting firm owner, applied this distinction to her client delivery model, she..." The distinction provides the intellectual value. The peer story provides the mimetic pressure. The prospect learns something genuinely useful AND encounters an internal mediator simultaneously.
- **The 90 distinctions opt-in becomes a mediator pipeline.** Everyone who opts in for the remaining 90 distinctions has self-selected as someone who found the first 10 valuable. They are now in a nurture sequence where each distinction can be paired with progressively stronger internal mediator stories -- from brief mentions in the free 10, to detailed before/after narratives in the paid-attention 90.
- **The sharing mechanic amplifies mediation.** When someone shares one of the free 10 distinctions with a colleague, they are also sharing the embedded peer story. The mediator reaches people who never encountered Strategic Profits directly. This is organic internal mediation at scale -- the peer story travels with the intellectual content.
- **Jay Abraham and Perry Marshall as "boundary mediators."** These are external mediators by Girard's framework (too distant for rivalry), but the fact that Rich used THESE SAME DISTINCTIONS when advising them creates a specific effect: the prospect thinks, "Jay Abraham needed these distinctions too?" This collapses the distance between the prospect and the external mediator. It does not make Jay Abraham an internal mediator -- but it makes the prospect feel that they are working with the same intellectual toolkit as the legends. That is a subtle but powerful form of proximity creation.

## Section 5: The "Business as Model" Play

Rich does not want to be the visible personality driving everything. Internal mediators are the structural solution to this preference.

### Client Success as Primary Content

Instead of Rich producing daily personal content (which he does not want to do), the content engine runs on client transformations:

- **Content volume problem solved.** Rich does not need to post daily. His clients' stories provide the raw material. A single client transformation can generate: one long-form case study, 3-5 social media posts, one email in the nurture sequence, one webinar segment, and one community highlight.
- **Authenticity problem solved.** Rich dislikes scripted content. Client stories are inherently authentic -- they are not Rich performing, they are real people describing real results.
- **Visibility transfer achieved.** When the content is "Strategic Profits client achieves X," the visibility accrues to Strategic Profits as an entity. Rich can remain behind the curtain while the brand gains recognition.

### Rich's Energy as the Collection Differentiator

Rich genuinely lights up when he sees someone have a breakthrough -- when they go from struggling with AI to building something that changes how their business operates. He loves the spark, the progression, the moment it clicks. That enthusiasm is not performable. It is the real thing. And it means Rich should be PRESENT for the internal mediator collection process, not delegated away from it.

When Rich interviews a client about their transformation, his authentic excitement about their progression creates content that no produced interview can match. The client feels seen. Rich's curiosity pulls out details a standard interviewer would miss. The energy in the conversation is palpable on camera, on audio, in a transcript. This is not a chore on Rich's calendar -- this is the kind of work he described as what he loves most: seeing the spark, building novel things, being at the edge of discovery.

The team handles logistics: scheduling, pre-interview research, recording setup, production, distribution. Rich shows up for the 30-minute conversation where someone describes the moment everything changed -- because that is the moment Rich's own energy becomes the differentiator that makes the content unmatchable.

### "Strategic Profits Client" as a Status Signal

The goal is to make "I'm a Strategic Profits client" carry the same weight in the AI-for-business market that "I'm a Two Comma Club member" carries in the funnel world.

How to build this:

1. **Create a recognition moment.** When a client completes Force Multiplier or Connect the Dots, give them something displayable -- a badge, a designation, a certificate with specific language. Not a generic "certificate of completion" but something tied to IDENTITY: "Strategic AI Architect" or "Force Multiplied" or whatever label crystallizes the transformation.

2. **Make the recognition shareable.** Design it for LinkedIn posts, email signatures, and social bios. Every display is an internal mediator radiating into the prospect's world.

3. **Feature recognized clients publicly.** An "Alumni" or "Client Showcase" section on the Strategic Profits website that prospects encounter during their buying journey. This is the "wall of winners" implemented digitally.

4. **Create alumni events or content.** Annual or quarterly gatherings (virtual or in-person) where past clients share what they have built. Prospects can observe these events (or see content from them), creating desire through exposure to a peer community operating at a higher level.

### The Community-as-Mediation Model

The highest form of internal mediation is not a testimonial. It is a community where being a member IS the proof:

- **Prospective buyers should be able to SEE the community before joining.** Not full access -- but enough to witness the caliber of people inside, the quality of discussion, and the level of results being shared. This creates the Girardian "scandal of the obstacle" -- the prospect is excluded from something desirable, which intensifies desire.
- **Existing members become recruiters by nature, not by incentive.** When a community member mentions their involvement in professional conversation ("I'm working on this with Rich Schefren's team through Force Multiplier"), they are deploying internal mediation organically. The listener, if they are a potential buyer, experiences immediate mimetic tension.
- **The Flagship ($50K) community should be the ultimate internal mediator.** The very existence of a group of entrepreneurs paying $50K/year to stay connected to Rich's operation signals: "There is a level above yours, and people at that level think this access is worth $50K." The prospect does not need to know what happens inside. The price and the participation of peers is enough.
