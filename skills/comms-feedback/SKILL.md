# Deliver Effective Feedback

Say hard things well. This skill creates feedback that drives improvement without damaging relationships.

## When to Use This Skill

- Performance conversations
- Peer feedback
- Client concerns
- Team member development
- Difficult conversations

## Required Inputs

- Context of feedback needed
- Specific behaviors/situations
- Impact observed
- Desired change
- Relationship context

## Output Format

```markdown
# Feedback Conversation Guide

**For:** [Recipient name]
**Context:** [Situation]
**Goal:** [What you want to achieve]

---

## Preparation

**Specific Situation:** [When/where this happened]
**Observable Behavior:** [What they did - facts only]
**Impact:** [Effect on others/project/business]
**Desired Change:** [What you want instead]

---

## Conversation Framework (SBI-I)

### Situation
"I wanted to talk about [specific situation/time/place]..."

### Behavior
"What I observed was [specific, factual behavior]..."

### Impact
"The impact of that was [consequence on you/team/project]..."

### Intent (Ask)
"Can you help me understand your perspective on this?"

### Way Forward
"Going forward, what I'd like to see is [specific change]..."

---

## Opening Script

[Natural opening that creates safety]

---

## Key Points to Make

1. [Point with example]
2. [Point with example]

---

## Anticipated Responses & Replies

**If they get defensive:**
"I hear that you see it differently. Can you help me understand your perspective?"

**If they make excuses:**
"I understand there were factors at play. What can we do differently next time?"

**If they shut down:**
"I can see this is hard to hear. I'm bringing this up because I want to see you succeed."

---

## Closing

"Thank you for hearing this. What support do you need to make this change?"

---

## Follow-Up Plan

**Check-in date:** [When]
**Success metric:** [How you'll know it's working]
```
