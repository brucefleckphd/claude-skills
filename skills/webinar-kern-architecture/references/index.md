## Master Systems (Ace/King)

### K1. Five-Phase Webinar Structure
**Tier:** 1 (Ace)
**Core Focus:** Confirmation → Difference → Bond → Payoff → Offer
**When to Use:** Understanding overall webinar presentation structure
**Key Insight:** Each phase builds on previous. Help first, sell second. Structure designed to be helpful throughout.

### K2. Webinar Mission Statement (Philosophy)
**Tier:** 1 (Ace)
**Core Focus:** Genuinely help, earn trust, foster relationships, make sales
**When to Use:** Understanding core philosophy behind Kern's approach
**Key Insight:** Long-term money comes from being genuinely beneficial. Order matters: Help → Relationship → Sales.

### K3. Webinar Campaign Process Map
**Tier:** 1 (King)
**Core Focus:** 7 sequential phases from Recon to Post-Webinar
**When to Use:** Planning complete campaign, understanding how pieces connect
**Key Insight:** Liquidator and post-webinar sequences are most overlooked strategic assets. Complete all 7 phases in 28 days.

## Core Frameworks (Queen/Jack)

### K4. A-to-Z Webinar Funnel Flow
**Tier:** 1 (Queen)
**Core Focus:** Complete flow from traffic source to purchase/re-invite
**When to Use:** Understanding campaign architecture from registration to purchase
**Key Insight:** When purchase happens anywhere in sequence, they exit sales flow and enter fulfillment. Prevents bombarding buyers.

### K5. Situation-Solution Diagnostic Framework
**Tier:** 1 (Queen)
**Core Focus:** Map each problem to its proven solution category
**When to Use:** Diagnosing webinar campaign problems, identifying which elements need optimization
**Key Insight:** Don't try to fix everything at once. Diagnose specific bottleneck and apply corresponding proven solution.

### K6. Recon Worksheet Three-Question Framework
**Tier:** 1 (Jack)
**Core Focus:** Top 3 desires, 3 WHYs, brainstorm objections, prioritize top 3
**When to Use:** At very beginning before creating any copy or offers
**Key Insight:** Complete this BEFORE writing anything. Answers inform copy angles, objection handling, offer structure, content.

### K7. Hybrid Webinar Model Rationale
**Tier:** 1 (Jack)
**Core Focus:** Pre-recorded presentation + Live Q&A
**When to Use:** Deciding between automated vs hybrid vs fully live
**Key Insight:** Hybrid beats automation in conversion (live Q&A), beats fully live in efficiency (perfect once).

### K8. Webinar Mission Statement (5 Goals)
**Tier:** 1 (Jack)
**Core Focus:** Responsive list, liquidator offset, trust/authority, genuine help, post-webinar sequence
**When to Use:** Defining success criteria for entire campaign
**Key Insight:** All 5 must be achieved. Missions 2 and 5 (liquidator, post-webinar) are financial accelerators most overlook.

## Supporting Frameworks (10)

### K9. Goodwill-to-Income Principle
**Tier:** 1 (10)
**Core Focus:** Bank balance = goodwill × offers made
**When to Use:** Deciding content vs pitch ratio, evaluating whether to teach in sales materials
**Key Insight:** From David Ogilvy. Every offer must contain value to maintain goodwill. Need both goodwill AND offers.

### K10. Bag of Tricks Problem-Solving Method
**Tier:** 2 (10)
**Core Focus:** Lookup table of proven solutions for common webinar problems
**When to Use:** When encountering webinar marketing problem, need quick proven solution
**Key Insight:** Don't experiment blindly. Match situation to solution column before trying custom approaches.

### K11. Overwhelm Prevention System (Chunking)
**Tier:** 2 (10)
**Core Focus:** Break everything into bite-sized 5-15 minute chunks
**When to Use:** Designing training programs or implementation systems
**Key Insight:** #1 impediment to progress for small businesses is overwhelm. Chunk down everything.

### K12. Big Picture Process Map
**Tier:** 1 (10)
**Core Focus:** Visual map with decision branches
**When to Use:** Understanding end-to-end Blueprint flow from traffic to post-webinar
**Key Insight:** Shows both ideal path and recovery sequences for non-ideal behaviors (didn't attend, didn't buy).

### K13. Revenue Benchmark Context
**Tier:** 3 (10)
**Core Focus:** $608,000 average per campaign over 2 years
**When to Use:** Setting expectations, evaluating if results are on track
**Key Insight:** Aspirational benchmark, not guarantee. Track progression: 1st covers costs, 2nd profits, 3rd+ scales.
