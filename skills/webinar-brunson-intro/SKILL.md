# Brunson Introduction Frameworks

Complete framework set for the first 5-7 minutes of your webinar—establishing the bold promise, commanding attention, qualifying yourself, and hooking to the end with level 10 energy throughout.

## When to Use This Skill

- Writing or improving webinar introductions
- Setting a measurable promise (the "ruler")
- Hooking attendees to stay through the end
- Commanding attention and eliminating distractions
- Establishing authority through qualification
- Managing energy levels throughout presentation
- Creating hope through future pacing

**Critical:** The first 5 minutes are THE retention battle. 90% of people who will leave do so in this window.

## Core Systems Explained

### The Bold Promise Ruler

**The Problem:** Attendees judge you against every other webinar they've experienced.

**The Solution:** Set a singular, specific, verifiable promise that becomes THE measurement standard. They judge only whether you fulfilled THIS promise, not how you compare to others.

**Example:** "I'm going to show you how to instantly add high ticket sales to your funnel without you ever personally talking on the phone to anyone."

**Quality Test:** Can they verify by webinar's end whether you delivered? If yes, it's a good ruler.

### The First 5-Minute Retention Window

**Critical Truth:** 90% of people who will leave your webinar do so in the first 4-5 minutes.

**Implication:** If you get them past this hump, they'll stay the full 90 minutes.

**Action:** Front-load ALL engaging elements in first 5 minutes:
- Bold promise
- End hook
- Command attention
- Energy at level 10
- Start qualification

First 5 minutes are not warm-up. They are THE battle.

### Hook to End Architecture

**Deploy Within:** First 5-7 minutes, immediately after bold promise

**What to Promise:** Something that is:
1. Exclusive (only at the end)
2. Valuable (worth waiting 85 more minutes for)
3. Unique (can't get elsewhere)
4. Specific (call center tour, iPad giveaway, bonus reveal, special pricing)

**Examples:**
- Behind-the-scenes tour via webcam
- Physical prize drawing
- Exclusive bonus stack reveal
- Special pricing available only to attendees who stay

**Test:** Would YOU stay 85 more minutes for this? If no, hook isn't strong enough.

### The Tonality and Energy Imperative

**Brunson's Core Principle:** When attendees can't see you, tonality carries ALL engagement.

**Required Energy:** Level 10/10 throughout entire presentation

**Physical Requirements:**
- Sit on edge of seat
- Move hands constantly
- Voice goes up really high and down low
- Extreme vocal variation

**The Paradox:** What feels "too much" in person sounds "just right" on webinar. Normal conversational energy reads as boring and causes people to leave.

**Test:** Record yourself. If you sound monotone or low-energy on recording, you're losing attendees in real-time.

### The Continuous Energy Arc

**Duration:** 90-120 minutes of sustained level 10 energy without break

**Phases:**
1. Pre-Start Loop: Level 10
2. Introduction: Level 10
3. Content: Level 10
4. Transition: Level 10
5. Close: Level 10

**Critical Rule:** Energy cannot dip at ANY point. Any dip creates exit point for attendees.

**Why It Works:** High energy maintains momentum that carries people through to purchase.

**Reality Check:** This is physically demanding. It's engineered performance, not natural conversation.

### Qualification Stack

**When:** After commanding attention, before transitioning to content

**What to Include:**
- Specific results (8 figures, not "I've done a lot")
- Social proof (specific client names)
- Time invested (years of experience)
- Unique positioning ("king of high ticket sales")
- Breadth of experience

**End With:** Declarative authority statement that claims expertise definitively.

**Pattern Interrupt:** Insert humor or unexpected element (Russell's sleeping picture) before bold authority claim. This breaks "bragging" perception and makes claim land better.

### The Hard Way vs Cheat Method

**Frame:** "I did it the hard way (struggle, failure, 10 years of testing, expensive mistakes) so you can have the cheat (proven template, shortcut, already-optimized system)."

**Value Creation:** Years compressed into hours. What took you 10 years to learn, they get in 90 minutes.

**Authenticity Requirement:** Must be truthful. If you actually spent 10 years, say so. If 6 months, say 6 months. Real struggle creates real value perception.

### Future Pacing

**When:** After qualification, transitioning from intro to content

**Method:** Guide attendees to visualize their future state AFTER achieving the outcome.

**Questions to Ask:**
- "Can you imagine what it's gonna be like when...?"
- "What would you do with your life?"
- "What would you do different?"

**Goal:** Engage emotion and imagination, not just logic. Create desire for the future state.

**Example:** "Can you imagine making high ticket sales every afternoon without ever picking up the phone? What would you do with your life? What would you do different?"

## The Tactical Sequence

```
Pre-Start Loop (2-4 min) →
Bold Promise Ruler (1 min) →
End Hook (1-2 min) →
Command Attention (30 sec) →
Qualification Stack (2-3 min) →
Future Pacing (1-2 min) →
[Transition to Content]
```

**Energy Level:** 10/10 throughout entire sequence

## Core Principles

1. **First 5 minutes = retention battle** - 90% leave or stay in this window
2. **Set the ruler** - One specific promise they judge you by
3. **Hook to end immediately** - Deploy within first 5 minutes
4. **Energy never dips** - Level 10 from pre-start through close
5. **Tonality carries everything** - When they can't see you, voice is everything
6. **Qualify with specifics** - Not generalities, specific proof points
7. **Future pace to create hope** - Make success feel achievable and desirable

## Brunson vs Fladlien: Key Differences

| Element | Fladlien | Brunson |
|---------|----------|---------|
| **Energy** | High throughout | Level 10 the ENTIRE time |
| **Authority** | Four Pillars + Testimonials | Qualification Stack + Pattern Interrupt |
| **Hook** | Mystery + Time Machine | End Hook + Bold Promise Ruler |
| **First 5 Min** | Authority + Commitment | Bold Promise + End Hook + Energy |
| **Philosophy** | Strategic sequencing | High energy + specific hooks |

**Key Insight:** Fladlien focuses on strategic layering of authority, commitment, objections, hope, and mystery. Brunson focuses on extreme energy, specific measurable promises, and powerful end hooks within the critical first 5 minutes.
