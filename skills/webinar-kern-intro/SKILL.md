# Kern Introduction Frameworks

Complete framework set for opening your webinar—the Confirmation phase (confirming their decision to attend) and The Difference phase (addressing skepticism and positioning yourself as 180 degrees different from the sleazy stereotype).

## When to Use This Skill

- Writing or improving webinar introductions
- Addressing buyer skepticism and objections early
- Differentiating yourself from negative market stereotypes
- Building credibility through story and damaging admissions
- Qualifying audience while creating safety and trust

**Important:** Kern's intro is built on radical honesty and being the OPPOSITE of what buyers expect from marketers.

## The Two-Phase Structure (Master Frameworks)

### PHASE 1: CONFIRMATION

**Three objectives:**

1. **CONFIRM** they made right decision to attend
   - Title slide with clear payoff (K3)
   - Preview of what you'll show them (K4)
   - List benefits they'll learn (K5)

2. **REESTABLISH FAMILIARITY** through brief introduction
   - They know you from indoctrination already
   - Use 'Known As' language (K8)
   - Credentialize through research or results (K9)

3. **SELL THEM ON STAYING** by future pacing
   - Why this is important NOW (K6)
   - Multiple 'Is this for you?' slides (K7)
   - Create excitement about what's coming

### PHASE 2: THE DIFFERENCE

**Two missions:**

1. **ACKNOWLEDGE SKEPTICISM** in non-combative way
   - All buyers have been mistreated (K23)
   - Nobody trusts marketers (K28)
   - Their objections are perfectly valid (K26)

2. **DO LIGHT TAKEAWAY** to filter and reassure
   - State who it's NOT for (K24)
   - House Rules with disqualifications (K25)
   - Major damaging admission in red (K26)
   - Confession of any unfair advantage (K27)

## The BOND Story Structure

Used within Phase 1 to create trust and bond with audience:

```
Initial Conflict → The Bottom → Turning Point → [Roadmap/Content] → Arrival
```

**Five elements:**

1. **INITIAL CONFLICT** (K12, K15)
   - Struggles before your discovery
   - Use Feel-Felt-Found OR Struggle-Loss-Discovery-Rebirth
   - Story setup: "If you've ever tried X but Y happened..."

2. **THE BOTTOM** (K16, K21)
   - Absolute lowest point
   - Make damaging admission to increase credibility
   - Must be true, relatable through emotion

3. **TURNING POINT** (K17)
   - Two doors decision: quit or commit to mastery
   - "I decided to roll up my sleeves and master this"
   - Frames success as choice, not luck

4. **ROADMAP** (content section)
   - The actual teaching/solution
   - Covered in payoff/content phase

5. **ARRIVAL** (K18)
   - Brief success state
   - Immediately pivot to serving them
   - "But who cares about that? How about I walk you through..."

## Core Principles

1. **Radical honesty beats hype** - Make damaging admissions competitors fear
2. **Be 180 degrees different** - From the sleazy marketer stereotype everyone expects
3. **Skepticism is normal** - Don't fight it combatively, work with it through transparency
4. **Takeaway creates desire** - Telling who it's NOT for makes right people want it more
5. **Story creates bond** - Use to address objections and show you understand their struggle
6. **Credentials through effort** - If no formal qualifications, credentialize through research/testing
7. **Align against problem** - Stand with buyer against what they hate (genuinely, not manipulatively)

## The Kern Differentiation Strategy

What sets Kern's intro apart:

**Traditional Approach:**
- Build yourself up
- Hide disadvantages
- Overcome objections combatively
- Promise everyone will succeed
- Be hypey to create excitement

**Kern's 180-Degree Opposite:**
- Make damaging admissions
- Acknowledge unfair advantages
- Address objections non-combatively
- State clearly who won't succeed
- Be radically honest to build trust

**Why it works:** Buyers expect sleazy marketing. When you're the opposite, you stand out dramatically and trust builds faster.

## Key Language Patterns

**Confirmation Benefits (K5):**
- "How to X so you can Y"
- "How to X without Y so you can Z"
- "How to avoid X so you can finally Y"
- "The new way to X"

**Story Transitions (K15):**
- "If you've ever tried X but Y happened, this is for you. Let me tell you a story."
- "If you've ever found [topic] overwhelming, I know exactly how you feel. We might have more in common than you think."

**Turning Point (K17):**
- "I had two choices. I could either [quit path] or I could [mastery path]. And I decided to..."

**Damaging Admission (K26):**
- "Most people who buy any how-to-make-money training will not make any money whatsoever."
- "I just threw big numbers at you. My results are not even remotely typical."

**Advantage Acknowledgment (K27):**
- "Confession: I have an advantage. [State it honestly]. Here's how I'll help you despite not having that..."

## Registration Page Optimization

Kern's proven strategies for high-converting registration pages:

### The Proven Template (K39)
**Webinar 2.0 Page, One Host, High CTA Button**
- Converts at ~59.5% (exceptionally high)
- Works with both Facebook ads and list traffic
- Outperforms other templates in real testing

### Key Elements

**Two-Step Opt-In (K37):**
- First click: Button ("Claim My Spot Now")
- Second click: Reveals form (name + email)
- Creates micro-commitment, reduces friction
- Button less intimidating than visible form

**Minimal Body Copy (K40):**
- Headline + bullets + form are what matter
- Section between bullets and form has minimal impact
- Template default or blank works fine
- Kern has only changed it once with no meaningful impact

**Remove Distractions (K41):**
- Turn off social media sharing buttons
- Every element should support ONE goal: registration
- Set Facebook visibility to 'hidden'
- Set Google Plus visibility to 'hidden'

**Platform Skepticism (K38):**
- Don't trust Leadpages "best converting" data blindly
- List relationship affects conversion MORE than template
- Test with YOUR traffic sources
- Kern's winner differs from platform's top template

**Thank-You Page Priority (K36):**
- MUST confirm registration BEFORE any pitch
- Anxiety about "Did it work?" blocks attention
- Standard: "Success! You're all set. Check email in 10 minutes..."
- Only pitch after they have peace of mind

**Alternative Platform (K42):**
- Megaphone App (by Kajabi team)
- 60+ templates, real-time stats
- Shows demographics (gender, age, marital status)
- Geographic data (where views/signups come from)
- Watch individual signups in real-time
