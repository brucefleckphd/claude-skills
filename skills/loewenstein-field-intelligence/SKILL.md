# Loewenstein Field Intelligence

---

## LAYER 1: INVARIANTS (Never override.)

1. **Freshness gate before synthesis.** Before producing the briefing, check the timestamps of all underlying Loewenstein reports. Any report older than 45 days is stale. Unlike Veblen (60-day window), behavioral dynamics shift faster — audience knowledge changes, visceral triggers lose novelty, empathy gaps shift as customers accumulate experience. If more than 2 reports are stale, refuse to synthesize and list which need refreshing.

2. **Convergence requires multiple independent signals.** A convergence zone must be confirmed by at least THREE independent Loewenstein skill outputs pointing at the same territory. Two signals = notable. Three or more = convergence zone. Name the zone only when the threshold is met.

3. **The Single Move is singular.** The highest-leverage behavioral intervention must be ONE thing — one curiosity gap to design, one empathy bridge to build, one visceral trigger to recalibrate, one information flow to restructure. Not a list. One move, with explicit reasoning for why it outranks everything else.

4. **Conflicts must be resolved, not listed.** When two skill outputs point in opposite directions (e.g., Curiosity Architect says "reveal more" but Anticipation Engine says "withhold for savoring"), analyze both, name the stronger signal, explain why, and make a recommendation.

5. **Every finding must cite its source skill.** All claims tagged with: [Curiosity Architect], [Empathy Gap Map], [Risk as Feelings], [Anticipation Engine], [Visceral Audit], [Information Flow]. Traceable to evidence.

6. **The self-examination findings get their own section.** Aggregate all self-examination findings from across the 6 reports into a single "Mirror" section. This is where the client confronts their behavioral blind spots — all in one place.

---

## LAYER 2: QUALITY GUIDANCE

7. **The convergence zones are the prize.** Individual skill findings are already in their reports. The convergence — what only becomes visible when looking at curiosity, empathy, risk, anticipation, visceral factors, and information flow simultaneously — is this skill's unique contribution.

8. **Temporal coherence check.** Review all temporal findings (from Anticipation Engine, Visceral Audit, and Empathy Gap Map) for consistency. Does the timing story make sense? Are visceral peaks aligned with anticipation peaks? Do empathy gap bridges fire at the right moments? Temporal misalignment across skills = structural problem.

9. **The Loewenstein-Girard-Veblen integration section activates when cross-thinker data exists.** If Girard and/or Veblen Field Intelligence briefings exist, cross-reference them. The three thinkers together answer the complete question: What does this market WANT (Girard), how does it SIGNAL what it has (Veblen), and what DRIVES or BLOCKS action on that wanting (Loewenstein)?

10. **Wanting/liking coherence check.** Review the wanting/liking balance from Information Flow alongside the empathy gap data and the visceral audit. Is the marketing creating wanting that the delivery can sustain? If three independent skills all flag a wanting/liking imbalance, that's a convergence zone requiring immediate action.

## PURPOSE

Each individual Loewenstein skill is a specialized analyst looking at one dimension of the market's behavioral field:
- **Curiosity Architect** — what information gaps exist and whether they drive attention or action
- **Empathy Gap Map** — where hot-cold state mismatches create churn, remorse, or disengagement
- **Risk as Feelings** — where emotional risk perception blocks action despite rational case
- **Anticipation Engine** — how temporal sequences build or squander anticipation
- **Visceral Audit** — which visceral factors drive behavior at each funnel stage
- **Information Flow** — how the overall information architecture serves curiosity, action, and satisfaction

This skill is the director who sits with all six analysts and asks: what does this mean TOGETHER? Where are multiple independent behavioral signals converging on the same problem or opportunity? What's the one move that addresses the most convergence zones simultaneously?

## EXECUTION PROCESS

### Step 1: Freshness Check

Locate all Loewenstein skill output reports in the project vault:
- `Loewenstein - Curiosity Architect/` → latest report
- `Loewenstein - Empathy Gap Map/` → latest report
- `Loewenstein - Risk as Feelings/` → latest report
- `Loewenstein - Anticipation Engine/` → latest report
- `Loewenstein - Visceral Audit/` → latest report
- `Loewenstein - Information Flow/` → latest report

Check file modification dates. Flag any older than 45 days. If more than 2 are stale, list which need refreshing and do not proceed.

### Step 2: Finding Extraction

From each report, extract:
- Top 3 findings (most impactful)
- Self-examination findings (blind spots)
- Cross-references to other thinkers
- Specific recommendations made

### Step 3: Convergence Detection

Look for convergence zones — territories where 3+ skills independently point at the same thing:
- **Same funnel stage flagged by multiple skills** (e.g., curiosity dies at the same point where visceral intensity drops and anticipation valleys)
- **Same audience behavior explained by multiple mechanisms** (e.g., low conversion explained by both risk-as-feelings AND empathy gap AND ostrich effect)
- **Same recommendation emerging from different analyses** (e.g., "the onboarding needs redesigning" surfaced by empathy gap, anticipation, AND information flow)

Name each convergence zone. Require 3+ sources. Explain what makes it a convergence, not just a coincidence.

### Step 4: Conflict Resolution

Identify where skills point in opposite directions:
- Curiosity Architect says "reveal more" but Anticipation Engine says "delay revelation"
- Visceral Audit says "increase emotional intensity" but Risk as Feelings says "reduce perceived risk"
- Empathy Gap Map says "cool the sale" but Information Flow says "the wanting is too low"

For each conflict: analyze both signals, determine which is dominant in this specific context, explain the reasoning, and make a definitive recommendation.

### Step 5: The Single Move

From all convergence zones, conflicts, and individual findings, determine the ONE highest-leverage behavioral intervention:
- What is it? (specific: "add a quick-win experience within 48 hours of purchase" — not vague: "improve onboarding")
- Why does it outrank everything else?
- Which convergence zones does it address?
- What is the expected impact?
- What is the implementation difficulty?

### Step 6: Mirror Section

Aggregate all self-examination findings from the 6 reports. Organize by theme:
- What is the client doing to themselves? (self-inflicted problems)
- What are they failing to see? (structural blind spots)
- What habit needs to break? (recurring error patterns)

### Step 7: Cross-Thinker Integration

If Girard and/or Veblen Field Intelligence briefings exist:
- **Girard × Loewenstein:** How do mimetic desire patterns interact with curiosity, visceral states, and risk perception?
- **Veblen × Loewenstein:** How do status dynamics interact with empathy gaps, anticipation, and information avoidance?
- **Three-way convergence:** Where do all three thinkers independently flag the same territory?

If no cross-thinker data exists, note the gap and recommend which thinker systems to run next.
