## PHASE 1: FOUNDATION & CONTEXT ANALYSIS

### 1.1 Product/Market Clarity

Based on user input, generate:

- **Refined positioning statement** (2 sentences)
- **Core value in emotional terms** — What transformation do they feel?
- **Job to be done** — What functional/emotional/social job does this fulfill?
- **Niche classification** — Specific market category

### 1.2 Market Segment Identification

Identify and describe:

**PRIMARY SEGMENT** (1 paragraph):
- The most valuable target market
- Geographic focus
- Demographic profile
- Company characteristics (if B2B)
- Estimated market size

**SECONDARY SEGMENT A** (2-3 sentences): Alternative high-value market

**SECONDARY SEGMENT B** (2-3 sentences): Emerging opportunity market

**SECONDARY SEGMENT C** (2-3 sentences): Adjacent/expansion market

*Continue all remaining analysis focused on PRIMARY SEGMENT.*

## PHASE 2: DEEP PERSONA ANALYSIS

### 2.1 Psychosocial Deep-Dive

**Geographic Context:**
- Physical and digital locations
- Communities they belong to
- Information sources

**Demographic Profile:**
- Age range, gender (if relevant)
- Income/budget level
- Education and expertise
- Job titles/roles (B2B)
- Company size/type (B2B)

**Psychographic Profile** — Bold headers, then visceral emotional expansion:

- **Identity & Self-Perception**: How do they see themselves?
- **Core Values**: What principles guide decisions?
- **Deepest Desires**: What do they crave at fundamental human level?
- **Status & Belonging**: How do they want to be perceived?
- **Control & Autonomy**: Where do they feel powerless/seek control?
- **Time Pressure**: How does time scarcity affect their experience?
- **Information Overwhelm**: How do they navigate complexity?
- **Risk Tolerance**: What keeps them safe vs. taking chances?
- **Trust Patterns**: Who/what do they trust? What creates skepticism?
- **Change Readiness**: How do they respond to new solutions?

### 2.2 Goals Framework

**PRIMARY GOAL**: State in future tense with active verb
*Example: "To systematically grow qualified pipeline without adding sales headcount"*

**Primary Goal Topic**: 1-2 word broad term

**Top 5 Secondary Goals**: Specific goals directly supporting primary goal

**Goal Descriptors (Micro-Promises)**: 5 compelling adverbs describing how you help achieve goals
- [Adverb] — [Brief explanation]

**Sample Positioning Statements**: 5 one-sentence statements using goal descriptors

### 2.3 Complaints & Pain Points

**Top 5 Primary Complaints**: Visceral emotional language describing most pressing frustrations

**Primary Complaint Topic**: 1-2 words

**Top 5 Secondary Complaints**: Related nagging problems and obstacles

**Secondary Complaint Topics**: 5 topics, 1-2 words each

### 2.4 Emotional Landscape

**Positive Feelings** (When Goals Achieved): 10 specific positive emotions

**Negative Feelings** (Current State): 10 current negative emotions

**Dreams & Aspirations** (The "Gigantic Why"):
- **Professional Dream**: What does career/business success look like?
- **Personal Dream**: How does success impact their life?
- **Identity Dream**: Who do they become when they succeed?
- **Legacy Dream**: What mark do they want to leave?
- **Freedom Dream**: What does liberation look like?

**Ultimate Fear**: Worst-case scenario if they don't achieve primary goal (3-5 sentences, emotional language, nightmare scenario)

### 2.5 Objections & Barriers

**Top 10 Objections**: What they refuse to do or won't tolerate in a solution

### 2.6 Root Causes Analysis

**Mistaken Beliefs → The Truth**: 10 mistaken beliefs preventing goal achievement, each with truth counter

Format:
> **Mistaken Belief**: [False belief]
> **The Truth Is**: [Reality and correction]

**The Enemy** (Sources of Misinformation):
- **Authority Figures**: What "experts" mislead them?
- **Social Circle**: What do friends/family/colleagues say?
- **Mental Models**: What ideas keep them from seeing solutions?
- **Internet Noise**: What does Google tell them that's wrong?

**Primary Cause Defined**: 2-3 paragraphs explaining THE core barrier preventing success with specific examples

**Top 5 Cause Triggers**: What causes the primary cause? Each with 3-5 sentence explanation + resolution strategy

**Consequences**: 10 negative consequences if they don't solve the primary cause
- Each with short story demonstrating impact (3-4 sentences, visceral language)

**Negative Statistics**: 5 frightening statistics about prevalence/impact with source citations and links

**Bad Habits**: 5 counterproductive behaviors with explanation of how each undermines success

### 2.7 Solutions Landscape

**False Solutions**: What have they tried that failed?
Format:
> Many [Avatar] are trying [False Solution], and it rarely works because: [Explanation + what happens instead + better alternative]

Include 3-5 false solutions.

**Success Myths**: 3 myths about what successful people are doing (with reality)

**Expensive Alternatives**: 10 costly options they might consider
- [Alternative — $X,XXX]: [Why it's problematic despite cost]

## PHASE 3: COMPETITIVE INTELLIGENCE

### 3.1 Competitor Identification

For **each of top 5 competitors**, provide:

- **Company Overview**: 2-3 sentences
- **Market Share**: Estimated %
- **Target Audience**: Who they serve
- **Core Products/Services**: What they offer
- **Pricing Strategy**: Price point and model
- **Unique Selling Propositions**: Key differentiators

**SWOT Analysis** for each:
- **Strengths**: 3-5 core competencies, unique resources, brand power
- **Weaknesses**: 3-5 limitations, vulnerabilities, negative perceptions
- **Opportunities**: 3-5 untapped markets, emerging trends, partnerships
- **Threats**: 3-5 market pressures, regulatory changes, disruptions

**Chain-of-Thought Connection**: How does this competitor's positioning relate to persona needs? What are they missing?

**USER'S PRODUCT/SERVICE SWOT**: Same comprehensive SWOT for user's product

### 3.2 Competitive Positioning Map

**Visual Positioning Framework** — 2x2 matrix with relevant axes:
- X-Axis: [e.g., Price Low ← → High]
- Y-Axis: [e.g., Simple ← → Complex]

**Positioning Plot**: Each competitor + user's product with quadrant and description

**Key Insights**:
- **Clusters**: Where are competitors grouped?
- **White Space**: Where are the gaps?
- **Opportunity**: Where should user position?

### 3.3 Feature Comparison Matrix

Create comprehensive comparison table.

**Feature Gap Analysis**: What high-importance features are underserved by competitors?

### 3.4 Pricing & Value Analysis

Pricing comparison with strategy recommendation based on value delivered, competitive positioning, and market dynamics.

### 3.5 Customer Sentiment Analysis

For each competitor:
- **Common Praises**: What customers love
- **Common Complaints**: What frustrates them
- **Sentiment Summary**: Overall perception
- **Persona Alignment**: How well does this match target's needs?

**Competitive Sentiment Insights**: Patterns and gaps in satisfaction

### 3.6 Marketing & Branding Evaluation

- **Messaging Analysis**: How competitors communicate
- **Channel Strategy Analysis**: What channels they use, what's working, what's underutilized
- **Brand Positioning Summary**: How user's brand should be perceived differently

## PHASE 4: MARKET GAP & OPPORTUNITY ANALYSIS

### 4.1 Trend Intersection Analysis

**Key Market Trends**: 5 trends with description and impact

**Trend Intersection Opportunities**: How trends combine to create new market spaces
- Intersection A: [Trend X + Trend Y] = [New opportunity]
- Intersection B: [Trend X + Trend Z] = [New opportunity]
- Intersection C: [Trend Y + Trend Z] = [New opportunity]

### 4.2 Persona Need-Gap Mapping

**Explicit Needs vs. Current Market Offerings**: Table format

**Implicit Needs** (Unarticulated): 5 needs persona doesn't know to ask for, with why it matters

### 4.3 Competitive Weakness Exploitation

For each top competitor weakness:
- **Persona Impact**: How does this weakness affect target buyer?
- **Exploitation Strategy**: How to address as competitive advantage?
- **Implementation**: Specific actions to capitalize

Cover 5-7 top weaknesses.

### 4.4 Blue Ocean Strategy Simulation

**Six Paths Framework Analysis**:

**Path 1: Look Across Alternative Industries**
- Current Industry Boundaries
- Alternative Industries solving similar problems
- Blue Ocean Opportunity

**Path 2: Look Across Strategic Groups**
- Strategic Groups in industry
- Blue Ocean Opportunity for new group

**Path 3: Look Across Chain of Buyers**
- Current Buyer Focus
- Alternative Buyer target
- Blue Ocean Opportunity from shift

**Path 4: Look Across Complementary Products/Services**
- What's used Before/During/After
- Blue Ocean Opportunity for bundle/integration

**Path 5: Look Across Functional/Emotional Appeal**
- Industry Standard (functional or emotional?)
- Blue Ocean Opportunity to flip appeal

**Path 6: Look Across Time**
- Current Trajectory
- Blue Ocean Opportunity to lead/redirect change

### 4.5 Disruptive Innovation Potential

**Low-End Disruption Opportunities**: Where could simpler, cheaper, "good enough" solution win?
- Opportunity, Target (early adopters), Strategy

**New-Market Disruption Opportunities**: Where could you create market that doesn't exist?
- Opportunity, Target (non-consumers to convert), Strategy

### 4.6 Value Innovation Mapping

**Industry Value Curve**: Current industry standard across key factors

**Value Innovation Strategy**:
- **ELIMINATE**: Factors to remove that industry takes for granted
- **REDUCE**: Factors to reduce below industry standard
- **RAISE**: Factors to raise above industry standard
- **CREATE**: Factors to create that industry never offered

### 4.7 Ecosystem Opportunity Analysis

**Current Ecosystem Map**:
- Upstream Partners (suppliers, platforms, enablers)
- Downstream Partners (distribution, service, support)
- Adjacent Players (complementary services)
- Platform Opportunities

**Partnership Opportunities**: 5 partner types with how partnership creates value for persona

**Ecosystem Play Strategy**: How ecosystem approach creates more compelling value proposition

### 4.8 Future Scenario Planning

**3 Scenarios** — Each with:
- Description (what happens in this future)
- Probability (High/Medium/Low)
- Impact on Persona (how needs change)
- Positioning Strategy (how to thrive)

**Robust Strategy Elements**: What strategic moves work across all scenarios?

## PHASE 10: FOLLOW-UP PROMPTS FOR DEEPER EXPLORATION

Provide these 15 copy-paste prompts:

1. **PERSONA DEEP-DIVE**: "Using the MarketSauce PRIME report, create a detailed day-in-the-life narrative for [PERSONA_NAME]. Include morning routine, work challenges, decision-making moments, emotional states, and evening reflections. Show where [PRODUCT_NAME] intersects with their real experience."

2. **COMPETITOR ANALYSIS**: "Analyze [COMPETITOR_NAME] in extreme detail. Research website, marketing materials, reviews, social media, recent announcements. Create comprehensive report on strategy, messaging, strengths, weaknesses, blind spots. Recommend 5 specific ways to outmaneuver them."

3. **CAMPAIGN DEVELOPMENT**: "Take Campaign Idea #[X] and develop complete campaign brief: creative concept, copywriting (headlines, body, CTAs), visual direction, channel executions, targeting, budget breakdown, success metrics, 30-day timeline."

4. **CONTENT CALENDAR**: "Create 90-day content calendar for [PRIMARY_CHANNEL] targeting [PRIMARY_SEGMENT]. Include specific topics, formats, posting schedule, repurposing strategy, engagement tactics. Each piece should address persona pain points from report."

5. **WORKSHOP BUILD-OUT**: "Develop complete workshop package for [WORKSHOP_NAME]: detailed outline with timing, slide deck content, workbook materials, promotional email sequence, registration page copy, social promotion plan, post-workshop conversion sequence."

6. **SALES MATERIALS**: "Create comprehensive sales enablement package: one-pager, pitch deck, objection handling guide, case study templates, ROI calculator, discovery call script. All materials should speak directly to [PRIMARY_SEGMENT]."

7. **EMAIL SEQUENCES**: "Develop three email nurture sequences: (1) Welcome for new subscribers, (2) Education for consideration stage, (3) Conversion for decision stage. Each email 200-300 words, reference persona insights, move toward [PRIMARY_CTA]."

8. **AD COPY VARIATIONS**: "Generate 25 ad copy variations for [PRIMARY_CHANNEL] targeting [PRIMARY_SEGMENT]. Include 5 angles based on different emotional triggers. For each angle, create 5 variations testing different formulas (PAS, AIDA, 4Ps, Before-After-Bridge, Problem-Agitate-Solve)."

9. **VIDEO SCRIPT SERIES**: "Create scripts for 5-video series targeting [PRIMARY_SEGMENT]. Each video: (1) Hook in 3 seconds, (2) Agitate pain point, (3) Present solution, (4) Provide value, (5) Clear CTA. Include shot descriptions, B-roll suggestions, on-screen text."

10. **MARKET RESEARCH UPDATE**: "Research latest trends, statistics, developments in [NICHE] over past 30 days. Find 10 recent articles/studies/announcements. Analyze how trends affect [PRIMARY_SEGMENT]. Identify new opportunities or threats not in original report."

11. **PARTNERSHIP STRATEGY**: "Identify 20 specific potential partners in [NICHE] ecosystem. For each: what they do, audience overlap, potential partnership model, mutual value exchange, outreach approach. Prioritize by likelihood of success and impact."

12. **OBJECTION HANDLING**: "Create comprehensive objection handling playbook addressing all objections from persona analysis. For each: empathetic acknowledgment, reframe, proof/evidence, story example, trial close. Make responses conversational."

13. **CASE STUDY FRAMEWORK**: "Develop case study template optimized for [PRIMARY_SEGMENT]: customer selection criteria, interview questions, story structure, metric highlights, quote integration, visual layout, distribution strategy. Outline 5 ideal case studies to pursue."

14. **CONVERSION OPTIMIZATION**: "Audit [WEBSITE_URL] through lens of [PRIMARY_SEGMENT]. Identify friction points, messaging gaps, missing trust signals, conversion barriers. Provide specific recommendations for homepage, product pages, pricing, checkout, post-purchase."

15. **BRAND STORYTELLING**: "Develop brand story framework connecting [PRODUCT_NAME] to [PRIMARY_SEGMENT]'s journey: origin story, mission/vision, values demonstration, customer transformation stories, founder narrative, future vision. Write in [BRAND_VOICE], structure for different formats."
