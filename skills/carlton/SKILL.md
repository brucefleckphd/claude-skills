## The 17-Point Simple Writing System

Carlton's complete system, developed over 30 years and 1-2 years to document:

### Phase 1: Research & Foundation (Steps 1-4)
1. **Market Research** - Competition, size of market, positioning
2. **Avatar** - The personification of your average prospect
3. **USP** - Unique Selling Proposition and positioning
4. **Features & Benefits** - Master list with "That Means" translation

### Phase 2: Core Elements (Steps 5-7)
5. **Bullets** - One-two punch fascination bullets
6. **Testimonials** - Short, Specific, Spicy third-party validation
7. **Hooks** - Incongruous juxtaposition of compelling sales elements

### Phase 3: Salesmanship (Steps 8-10)
8. **Salesmanship Mentality** - Don't sell from your heels, be the Go-To Guy
9. **Voice & Story** - Develop your unique voice and compelling story
10. **Headlines** - Headline flows from hook, gets them to read first line

### Phase 4: Structure (Steps 11-12)
11. **Sales Funnel** - Campaign type, delivery system, offer structure
12. **Sales Message Basics** - Who I am, what I've got, why it matters, what to do

### Phase 5: Persuasion & Close (Steps 13-17)
13. **Huge Promise** - Truthful, big promise that sets up opportunity
14. **Reverse All Risk** - Guarantees and objection handling
15. **Turbulence** - Urgency, limitations, takeaway, opportunity
16. **Knock 'Em Off The Fence** - Bonuses and final push
17. **Your Close** - The money shot, assume the sale, first person

## Carlton's Core Principles

### The Big 3 Rules

1. **Don't Sell From Your Heels** - Get straight on your goal. There is money out there that belongs to you. If you have the goods, shame on you if you don't do all you can to get your message across.

2. **Be The Go-To Guy** - The opposite of selling from your heels. Say "I have what you need." Position yourself as THE authority.

3. **Wake Them From The Zombie State** - Your prospect is in a dead, somnambulant blob state. You have seconds to jolt them awake. Be the one thing today that shocks them out of their zombie state.

### The One-Two Punch

People have TWO reasons for buying:
1. **Rational reasons** - To explain the purchase to spouse, neighbors, brother-in-law
2. **Emotional A-Brain reasons** - The real reasons that seal the deal deep inside

You must address BOTH. Satisfy the rational need with proof and logic. Trigger the emotional A-Brain with greed, fear, ego, and primal desires.

### Voice Requirements

- **Bar Room Conversation** - Write like you're explaining it to a friend in a bar
- **First Person** - By the close, they should feel like they know you
- **Specific, Not General** - "I'm a parent with three kids, one in jail and two in kindergarten" not "I'm a parent"
- **No Throat Clearing** - Cut the first paragraph or page if it's just warming up

### The So What? Test

After every paragraph, ask: "So what?" Why should your prospect care about this particular section? If you can't answer, cut it or rewrite it.

## CRITICAL: The Hook-First Rule

**THIS IS NON-NEGOTIABLE. VIOLATION = COPY FAILURE.**

The hook is "the one step that, once you get it down, will carve out a spot for you in any market." The hook must:

1. Use **incongruous juxtaposition** of compelling sales elements
2. **Immediately transition to credibility** and believability
3. Bring them out of THEIR world into YOUR world
4. Shock them out of zombie state within seconds

### Incongruous Juxtaposition Examples:
- "Skinny little golf genius" + "425-yard tee shots" = HOOK
- "One-legged golfer" + "adds 50 yards to drives" = HOOK
- "Out-of-shape 55 year-old" + "humiliates PGA pros" = HOOK
- "Weak guy beats strong guy" = HOOK
- "Poor guy discovers easy way to get rich" = HOOK

**If your hook doesn't create cognitive dissonance, STOP and fix it.**

**Absorbed from Clayton (1 win - skeptics):**
- **Nuclear Guarantee Architecture** - For burned audiences, standard guarantee isn't enough
- Triple-layer: Money back + time compensation + keep bonuses
- Add to Step 14: Proof stacking BEFORE guarantee reveal

**Absorbed from Match 6 (Deutsch won Technical Complex - 91):**
- **Story-First Identity Transformation** - Hide method inside story, make buyer the hero
- **"Live Demo Scene"** - Show the emotional moment when solution clicks (not just results)
- **Trust-Building Voice** - Confiding, not selling. Method visibility = trust break.
- **Why this matters for Carlton:** Bar room voice is good, but showing the "Simple Writing System" breaks trust. Story must HIDE the method.

**Absorbed from Match 7 (Evaldo won Constraint Conflict - 91):**
- **Cohort Study Proof** - Carlton scored 79 (4th), but adapted voice successfully. Add longitudinal data to strengthen proof.
- **Voice Calibration Validated** - "$47M man losing sleep" worked as elevated incongruous juxtaposition. BUT some phrases ("financially pickpocketed") still edge toward compliance risk. Soften language without losing punch.
- **Length Discipline** - Carlton's 2,019 words was shortest = most respectful of executive time. KEEP this discipline.
- **Why Carlton Lost:** Proof structure less robust than Evaldo's cohort study. Numbers presented as CLAIMS not EVIDENCE.
