# Mechanism Evaluator Rubrics

> 13-dimension V3 Scorecard with detailed scoring criteria for each dimension.

---

## Scoring Thresholds

| Score | Rating | Verdict |
|-------|--------|---------|
| 0-49 | **Weak** | Needs complete rework |
| 50-69 | **Below Average** | Major improvements needed |
| 70-89 | **Average** | Competitive but not dominant |
| 90-109 | **Strong** | Competitive, likely to win |
| 110-119 | **Excellent** | Domination potential |
| 120-130 | **Elite** | Category-defining |

---

## Dimension Rubrics

### 1. Novelty (1-10)
- **1-3:** "I've heard this before"
- **4-6:** "Interesting angle"
- **7-9:** "I never thought of it that way"
- **10:** "This changes everything"

### 2. Specificity (1-10)
- **1-3:** Generic, unnamed concepts
- **4-6:** Somewhat specific, partial naming
- **7-9:** Fully named, concrete, measurable
- **10:** Vivid, unique, proprietary naming

### 3. Scientific Credibility (1-10)
- **1-3:** No evidence cited
- **4-6:** General claims, weak sources
- **7-9:** Specific studies, named researchers
- **10:** Published research, institutional backing

### 4. Visual Clarity (1-10)
- **1-3:** Abstract, hard to picture
- **4-6:** Partial imagery
- **7-9:** Clear visual metaphor
- **10:** Unforgettable image, highly shareable

### 5. Emotional Resonance (1-10)
- **1-3:** Flat, no feeling
- **4-6:** Mild interest
- **7-9:** Strong emotional response
- **10:** Visceral reaction (fear, hope, anger, relief)

### 6. Enemy Clarity (1-10)
- **1-3:** No clear villain
- **4-6:** Vague enemy
- **7-9:** Named, specific villain
- **10:** Hateable enemy with origin story

### 7. Inevitability (1-10)
- **1-3:** No urgency
- **4-6:** Some pressure
- **7-9:** Strong urgency, accumulating threat
- **10:** "Act now or suffer consequences"

### 8. Solution Elegance (1-10)
- **1-3:** Complicated, confusing
- **4-6:** Understandable but complex
- **7-9:** Simple, direct, clear path
- **10:** "Of course! Why didn't I think of that?"

### 9. Unique Ownership (1-10)
- **1-3:** Anyone could claim this
- **4-6:** Somewhat differentiated
- **7-9:** Hard to copy
- **10:** Only they can claim this

### 10. Paradigm Shift Power (1-10)
- **1-3:** Confirms existing beliefs
- **4-6:** Slight reframe
- **7-9:** Significant perspective shift
- **10:** "Everything I knew was wrong"

### 11. False Solution Destruction (1-10)
- **1-3:** Doesn't explain why others failed
- **4-6:** Partial explanation
- **7-9:** Clear why other solutions failed
- **10:** Completely explains solution failure

### 12. Market Sophistication Calibration (1-10)
- **1-3:** Too simple or too complex for market
- **4-6:** Slightly off-calibrated
- **7-9:** Good market fit
- **10:** Perfect calibration for exact market

### 13. Proof Integration (1-10)
- **1-3:** No proof, just claims
- **4-6:** Some proof, weakly integrated
- **7-9:** Strong proof, well integrated
- **10:** Proof woven throughout, undeniable

---

## Scoring Interpretation

### Score 120-130 (Elite)
**Elite mechanisms are:**
- Category-defining
- Copy-ready immediately
- Multiple industries possible
- Strong competitive moat
- Million-dollar control potential

### Score 110-119 (Excellent)
**Excellent mechanisms:**
- Strong competitive advantage
- Deep research backing
- Unique ownership
- Likely to dominate
- Ready for testing

### Score 90-109 (Strong)
**Strong mechanisms:**
- Competitive and differentiated
- Good market fit
- Solid proof integration
- Likely to convert
- May need minor improvements

### Score 70-89 (Average)
**Average mechanisms:**
- Workable but not dominant
- Missing novelty or proof
- Need improvements before launch
- Competitive but not category-defining

### Score 50-69 (Below Average)
**Below average mechanisms:**
- Significant rework needed
- Major gaps in proof/novelty
- Consider alternative approaches
- Not ready for testing

### Score 0-49 (Weak)
**Weak mechanisms:**
- Complete rework required
- Not viable in current form
- Recommend starting over
- Fails multiple quality tests
