# Practitioner Persuasion - Voice Patterns Guide

## The Core Voice

**Identity:** Expert peer sharing intelligence
**Energy:** Calm confidence with strategic intensity bursts
**Relationship:** Colleague to colleague

---

## Subject Line Voice (HIGH INTENSITY)

### Patterns That Work

**Incongruous Juxtaposition:**
- "Your happiest patients are your biggest problem"
- "The expertise trap killing your practice"
- "Why your best month might be your worst sign"

**Data Consequence Hooks:**
- "23% of practices missed this shift"
- "The 2024 patient trend that's accelerating"
- "What the top 5% changed last quarter"

**Challenge Questions:**
- "Still marketing like it's 2019?"
- "Wondering where your referrals went?"
- "Is your expertise actually hurting you?"

### What to Avoid in Subjects
- "FREE" or "URGENT" (spam triggers)
- "I have a question" (too vague)
- Long subjects (under 8 words ideal)
- Hyperbole ("revolutionary", "amazing")

---

## Opening Voice (LOW INTENSITY)

### Patterns That Work

**Peer Opening:**
- "Between practitioners..."
- "I've been noticing something in practices like yours..."
- "Here's what the data is showing us..."

**Observation Opening:**
- "There's a pattern I keep seeing..."
- "Something changed in [year] that most practices missed..."
- "I don't know if this applies to you, but..."

**Shared Challenge Opening:**
- "The referral game isn't what it used to be..."
- "Patient acquisition got harder this year..."
- "Marketing as a practitioner is genuinely difficult..."

### What to Avoid in Openings
- Pitching (too early)
- Credentials-first (earn the right to credentials)
- "I'm reaching out because..." (vendor voice)
- Long company intros

---

## Body Voice (MEDIUM INTENSITY)

### Transition Patterns

**Building Logic:**
- "The reason this matters..."
- "What that means for a practice like yours..."
- "Here's the part most people miss..."

**Creating Curiosity:**
- "The counterintuitive thing is..."
- "But here's where it gets interesting..."
- "Most practitioners assume... but the data shows..."

**Establishing Credibility:**
- "In working with [X] practices, I've seen..."
- "The research on this is pretty clear..."
- "Here's what the top [X]% are doing differently..."

### Proof Cascade Voice

**Data Proof:**
"The numbers are pretty stark: [specific statistic]"

**Peer Proof:**
"Practices that have made this shift are seeing..."

**Logic Proof:**
"It makes sense when you think about it - [logical chain]"

**Results Proof:**
"One practice I worked with went from [X] to [Y]..."

---

## Close Voice (LOW INTENSITY)

### CTA Patterns

**Soft Ask:**
- "If this resonates, worth a quick conversation?"
- "Curious if the pattern fits your practice?"
- "Worth 15 minutes to explore?"

**No-Pressure Ask:**
- "No commitment, just a diagnostic..."
- "Not a pitch - just seeing if there's a fit..."
- "Happy to share what I'm seeing either way..."

**Alternative Close:**
- "Either way, keep an eye on [trend]..."
- "Whether we talk or not, [valuable tip]..."
- "At minimum, worth tracking [metric]..."

### What to Avoid in Closes
- "Limited time" (pressure language)
- "Act now" (urgency without data backing)
- Multiple CTAs (confusing)
- New information (close is for closing)

---

## Signature Voice

### Positioning Elements

**Good:**
- Name only (clean)
- Name + single credential
- Name + brief descriptor

**Avoid:**
- Long titles
- Multiple credentials
- Company boilerplate
- Links galore

### Example Signatures

```
Best,
[Name]
[Single relevant credential]
```

```
[Name]
Practice Growth Strategist | [Credential]
```

| Section | Intensity | Selling | Trust-Building |
|---------|-----------|---------|----------------|
| Subject | 10/10 | 0/10 | 3/10 |
| Opening | 3/10 | 0/10 | 10/10 |
| Body | 6/10 | 3/10 | 8/10 |
| Close | 4/10 | 5/10 | 9/10 |

---

## Red Flags in Voice

### Marketing Speak (Avoid)
- "Revolutionary"
- "Game-changing"
- "Secrets"
- "Exclusive"
- "Guaranteed"

### Vendor Voice (Avoid)
- "We help practices..."
- "Our solution..."
- "Let me tell you about our..."
- "I'm reaching out on behalf of..."

### Pressure Voice (Avoid)
- "Act now"
- "Limited time"
- "Don't miss out"
- "You can't afford to wait"

---

## Voice Self-Test

Read your draft aloud. Answer:

1. Does it sound like one professional talking to another?
2. Would you say this to a colleague you respect?
3. Is there any moment that triggers "I'm being sold to"?
4. Does the subject grab attention without feeling spammy?
5. Does the opening earn trust before pitching anything?
6. Does the close make action feel safe, not urgent?

If any answer is wrong, revise that section.
