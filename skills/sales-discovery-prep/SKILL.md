# Prepare for Discovery Call

Walk into every call prepared. This skill creates comprehensive discovery prep including research, questions, and objection handling.

## When to Use This Skill

- Before initial discovery calls
- Preparing for qualification meetings
- Demo preparation
- Renewal/expansion conversations

## Required Inputs

- Prospect company and contact
- Meeting time and duration
- What you know so far
- Your objective for the call
- Products/services to potentially discuss

## Instructions

1. **Research review** - Pull together all known intel
2. **Build agenda** - Structure the conversation
3. **Craft questions** - Layer from surface to deep
4. **Prepare value hooks** - Stories for likely pain points
5. **Plan next step ask** - Know your CTA before you call

## Call Agenda

### Opening (2-3 min)
- Thank them for time
- Confirm agenda and time
- State your goal for the call

### Discovery (15-20 min)
- Current situation questions
- Problem exploration
- Impact quantification
- Decision process

### Value Demonstration (10 min)
- Relevant case study
- How you could help
- Initial solution ideas

### Next Steps (5 min)
- Summarize key points
- Propose next step
- Confirm timeline

---

## Discovery Questions

### Situation (Current State)
1. "Walk me through how your team currently handles [relevant process]?"
2. "What tools/solutions are you using today?"
3. "How long have you been doing it this way?"

### Problem (Pain Points)
1. "What's working well? What's frustrating?"
2. "If you could wave a magic wand, what would change?"
3. "How does this impact [relevant metric/outcome]?"

### Impact (Business Impact)
1. "When this problem happens, what's the ripple effect?"
2. "What does this cost you - in time, money, or opportunity?"
3. "How does this affect your team's ability to [achieve goal]?"

### Need-Payoff (Solution Value)
1. "If we could solve [problem], what would that mean for you?"
2. "What would success look like 6 months from now?"
3. "How would your day-to-day change?"

### Decision Process
1. "Walk me through how you typically evaluate new solutions?"
2. "Who else would need to weigh in on something like this?"
3. "What's your timeline for making a change?"
