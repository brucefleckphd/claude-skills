# Brunson Transition Frameworks

Complete framework set for transitioning from teaching to selling—Russell's permission-based approach that eliminates resistance.

## When to Use This Skill

- Creating the transition section of your webinar
- Moving from content to pitch without awkwardness
- Eliminating fear and nervousness about selling
- Getting the audience to psychologically consent to being sold to
- Overcoming the "awkward moment" between teaching and offering

**Core Insight:** Don't just shift to selling—ask permission and let them invite the offer. They'll literally ask you to sell them.

## How to Use

**Primary Framework: The Transition Permission Pattern**

Use this 5-step sequence at the transition point (typically 45-60 minutes in):

1. **Acknowledge the value** - "This stuff's been pretty cool, right? You've learned some good stuff."
2. **Acknowledge time limits** - "But an hour is not that long."
3. **Ask permission** - "How would you guys like to work with me directly where I can actually help you implement what we just talked about?"
4. **Wait for invitation** - Let them respond in chat: "yes, yes, please, I want help"
5. **Get explicit consent** - "I know it's always awkward like you're on this webinar and I do have something to sell and if you guys are okay with that I'd love to share with you."

**Supporting Frameworks: Fear Diagnostics**

If you experience any of these symptoms during your transition, you're in the fear pattern:
- Speaking faster
- Getting nervous
- Face turning red
- Starting to sweat
- Mumbling
- Rushing through the offer
- Awkward ending without clear CTA

**The fix:** Use the Permission Pattern structure. It eliminates fear by letting THEM ask YOU to sell.

## The Permission Pattern Explained

**Why This Works:**

Traditional approach: "Okay, so now let me tell you about my offer..."
- Abrupt shift creates resistance
- You feel like you're imposing
- They feel "sold to"
- Sales resistance goes up

Permission approach: "How would you guys like to work with me directly?"
- They ASK you to share
- You feel invited to help
- They feel in control
- Resistance drops to zero

**The Psychology:**

When you ask permission and they say "yes, share, tell me," they've psychologically consented. They WANT to hear your offer. You're not imposing—you're responding to their request.

## The Fear Pattern (What NOT to Do)

Russell's MLM Party story illustrates the fear pattern perfectly:

The presenter was amazing during content—passionate, fired up, excellent teaching. Then as she approached the pitch:
- Started talking faster
- Got nervous
- Face turned red
- Started sweating
- Started mumbling
- Rushed through: "Oh, and by the way, you can make money, blah, blah, blah..."
- Ended awkwardly

**Why this happens:** The presenter loved teaching but feared selling. Without a structured transition method, fear took over and destroyed the close.

**The solution:** The Permission Pattern eliminates this fear because you're not "selling"—you're responding to their request to help them implement.

## Core Principles

1. **Ask permission** - Don't just transition; get invited
2. **Let them request it** - Wait for them to say "yes, share, tell me"
3. **Acknowledge the awkwardness** - "I know it's always awkward... I do have something to sell"
4. **Watch for fear symptoms** - Speed increase = fear pattern activated
5. **Structure eliminates fear** - When they ask you to sell, you're serving not imposing

## Common Mistakes

**Mistake #1:** Abrupt shift to selling
- Going directly from teaching to "Here's my offer..."
- Skips the permission step
- Creates resistance

**Mistake #2:** Apologizing for selling
- "Sorry, but I do have something to sell..."
- Shows you're in fear pattern
- Undermines your authority

**Mistake #3:** Rushing the transition
- Speaking faster as you approach the pitch
- Physical symptom of fear
- Telegraphs discomfort

**Mistake #4:** Not waiting for response
- Asking permission but not pausing
- Not letting them invite you
- Defeats the purpose

## Validation Questions

**Before using this transition, check:**
- Have I delivered valuable content in the last 45-60 minutes?
- Am I about to rush or speak faster? (If yes, use Permission Pattern)
- Will I let them respond and invite the offer?
- Am I comfortable acknowledging "I have something to sell"?

**After using this transition, check:**
- Did they literally ask me to share in the chat?
- Do I feel invited rather than imposing?
- Has resistance dropped?
- Am I in a confident state to present the offer?

## The "Boom, That's It" Moment

Russell's description of the Permission Pattern ending: "**Boom, that's it**... People like yeah share me share share and they start asking you to sell them okay and it becomes really easy."

This is the goal: They're literally typing "share, yes, tell me" in the chat. You're not selling—you're responding to their request. The psychological shift is complete.
