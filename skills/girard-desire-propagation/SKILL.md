# Girard Desire Propagation Tracker

---

## LAYER 1: INVARIANTS (Never override.)

1. **Desires not trends.** This tool tracks what the market is *wanting*, not what they're *talking about*. Someone can talk about AI without wanting AI tools. The signal is in aspiration language: "I need to get on this," "I can't afford to miss this," "look what she built," "I feel behind." Trend analysis is not this tool.

2. **Velocity requires comparison.** A desire is "rising" or "fading" relative to a previous period. If this is the first run, produce the baseline report and label it as such. On subsequent runs, compare against prior reports in the output folder.

3. **Sources for each desire.** Every desire identified must include: who introduced it to the market, how it spread, and where it currently sits in the propagation curve. Anonymous "the market wants X" without traceable source = invalid finding.

4. **Desire conflicts must be named explicitly.** When two incompatible desires are propagating simultaneously (e.g., "be the solo operator" vs. "build a team empire"), name the conflict explicitly. These internal conflicts are the most valuable strategic intelligence — your marketing can resolve tensions competitors ignore.

5. **Live research required.** Minimum 50 sources scanned per run. Sources must include at minimum: 2 social platforms, 3 newsletters/Substacks, 1 podcast recap source, 1 community forum. Training data is not sufficient.

---

## LAYER 2: QUALITY GUIDANCE

6. **Desire gaps are the prize.** The most valuable finding is a Desire Gap — something the market logically wants based on what's rising, but that no model has yet articulated. This is where the clearest positioning opportunity lives. Give Desire Gaps the most attention in strategic recommendations.

7. **Distinguish desire from anxiety.** Anxiety ("I'm worried about AI replacing my business") is not the same as desire ("I want to be the person who uses AI to dominate"). They can coexist but have different marketing implications. Anxiety needs resolution. Desire needs modeling. Name both but distinguish them.

8. **Lead time is the value.** The point of this tool is 2-4 months of lead time over competitors who read trend reports. Frame findings in terms of what the market will be wanting by the time competitors notice it today.

---

## PURPOSE

Markets don't shift all at once. Desires propagate through populations in waves — starting with a few models who visibly want something, spreading to early imitators, then accelerating as the desire becomes visible, then fading as it saturates. This tool maps exactly where every significant desire is in that propagation curve for your specific market.

**Why this changes strategy:** Most marketers react to desire trends after they're already obvious — which means they're competing in saturated territory. This tool detects desires 2-4 months before saturation so you can be the model who introduces the desire to your core market before competitors arrive.

## OUTPUT STRUCTURE: Desire Velocity Report

**Header:**
- Market: [definition]
- Report period: [date range]
- Previous report: [link if exists, or "Baseline run"]
- Sources scanned: [count]

---

**Section 1: Rising Desires (Gaining Momentum)**

For each rising desire (target: 5-8 per report):
- **Desire statement** — What specifically the market is wanting (1-2 sentences, concrete)
- **Velocity score** — 1-10 (10 = spreading rapidly across many sources)
- **Origin** — Who/what introduced this desire and when
- **Propagation path** — How it spread (single viral post? Gradual emergence? Event trigger?)
- **Current stage** — Early (few models only), Building (spreading to early majority), Peaking (visible everywhere), Saturating (everyone is saying it = it's becoming noise)
- **Evidence** — 3+ specific quotes with sources showing this desire in the wild
- **Positioning opportunity** — How Rich/Strategic Profits could position as the model for this desire

---

**Section 2: Desire Sources (Who Is Driving What)**

For the top 5 rising desires: who introduced them and who is amplifying them. Identifies whether the origin is:
- Single-model origin (one person started it — viral risk, fast spread, but fragile)
- Multi-model simultaneous emergence (organic market shift — slower but more durable)
- Event-triggered (conference, news event, product launch — creates spike then decline)

---

**Section 3: Desire Conflicts (Incompatible Desires Propagating Simultaneously)**

For each active conflict:
- **Desire A** vs. **Desire B** — the incompatible wants
- **Who holds each** — different segments of the market, or same people at different moments?
- **Tension evidence** — quotes showing people expressing both desires or arguing about them
- **Resolution opportunity** — how Rich's positioning or content could explicitly resolve the tension and claim both audiences

---

**Section 4: Fading Desires (Losing Momentum)**

For each fading desire (target: 3-5):
- **Desire statement** — What the market was wanting that is now declining
- **Peak period** — When this was at maximum intensity
- **Fade signals** — Evidence the desire is losing momentum
- **Competitor exposure** — Which competitors are still marketing to this desire (and therefore falling behind)
- **Strategic implication** — If Rich is marketing to this desire, flag for repositioning

---

**Section 5: Desire Gaps (The Prize)**

For each desire gap (target: 3-5):
- **The gap** — A desire that is logically implied by what's rising but that no model has yet explicitly articulated
- **Evidence it exists** — Oblique signals (search questions, frustrated comments, "someone should..." posts)
- **Gap quality score** — 1-10 (based on evidence density and strategic fit for Rich)
- **How to claim it** — Specific content or positioning moves that would make Rich the model for this desire before competitors notice

---

**Section 6: Strategic Implications for Rich Schefren / Strategic Profits**

Synthesizes findings into 3-5 specific recommendations:
- Which rising desires to align with immediately
- Which desire gaps to move on before saturation
- Which desires to exit (fading — stop spending budget here)
- What desire conflict(s) Rich could resolve to capture both sides
- Specific content angle or product positioning triggered by this report

---

## RESEARCH PROCESS

### Sources to scan (minimum):

**Social Platforms:**
- Twitter/X: searches for aspiration language in the target market ("I need to," "finally figured out," "I can't afford not to," "this changed everything") combined with market-specific terms
- LinkedIn: articles and posts from target market cohort, comment sections on high-engagement posts

**Newsletters/Substacks:**
- 5-10 newsletters read by the target market, looking for "what I'm excited about," "what I'm noticing," "the shift I'm seeing" language

**Podcast Transcripts/Recaps:**
- Episode titles and descriptions from 3-5 podcasts the target market listens to
- Guest booking patterns (who is getting booked = what desires they mediate)

**Community Forums:**
- Reddit communities, Slack workspaces, Facebook groups, Skool communities
- "What are you working on?" threads, "What's changed for you lately?" discussions

**Book/Course Launches:**
- New book launches in the market (what desires they're positioning to)
- New course/program launches (what transformation they're promising)

**Event Recaps:**
- Conference takeaways, summit summaries, retreat recaps posted publicly
