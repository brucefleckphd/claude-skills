# Signature Talk Architect

## Overview

A complete architecture system for creating the ONE presentation an expert gives anywhere — to any version of their target audience — that teaches genuine value, shifts beliefs, and positions an offer as the logical next step.

**Core principle:** A signature talk is a belief-shifting conversation, not a product pitch or lecture.

**Emotional arc:** Recognition → Reframe → Resolution → Action

---

## When to Use

- Expert needs a flagship talk for conference, podcast, webinar, or workshop
- Speaker wants to stop building new decks for each venue (one talk, refined over 50+ deliveries)
- Need to convert a "value dump" presentation into a single-distinction talk
- Building a talk that positions WITHOUT feeling like a sales pitch
- Creating a close module for a talk that IS selling from stage

**When NOT to use:**
- Writing copy (use Carlton, Clayton, Evaldo, or Deutsch skills)
- Building a webinar sequence (use webinar-expert or webinar-brunson)
- Creating course content (different structure)

## The 4-Act Architecture

### Act 1: The Confession (Minutes 0–5)
**Purpose:** Build trust through vulnerability. "This person is like me."

- Open with a pattern interrupt (NOT "Hi, I'm [name]...")
- Share the failure/frustration moment (from your story)
- Three beats: setup → failure → emotional truth
- Energy: medium-low, conversational

### Act 2: The Reframe (Minutes 5–12)
**Purpose:** Introduce the ONE core distinction. Make them see the problem differently.

- State the distinction clearly — this is the centerpiece of the entire talk
- Use a metaphor that makes it visual and sticky
- Three proof points: personal example, client story, audience's own experience reframed
- The "You've Been Doing This" moment — they feel it in their gut
- Energy: building, more animated

### Act 3: The Framework (Minutes 12–22)
**Purpose:** Teach your proprietary methodology. Demonstrate depth of thinking.

- Name it, show it, make it feel like insider knowledge
- 3–5 teachable components (each: one-sentence definition, quick example, connection to distinction)
- **The Diagnostic Moment** — live self-assessment using lead magnet questions (most powerful part)
- **Quick Win** — one specific action they can take this week
- Energy: teaching pace, slower and clearer with pauses

### Act 4A: The Positioning Close (Minutes 22–28) — when NOT selling
- Paint the vision of life after implementing the framework (be specific)
- The Line in the Sand — genuine fork in the road, not a guilt trip
- Frame the next step as logical continuation, not pitch
- Close by circling back to the opening confession

### Act 4B: The Close Module (Minutes 22–35) — when selling from stage
- Bridge: "Everything I just showed you, I've turned into a complete system..."
- The Stack (each component maps to a problem from Acts 1–3)
- Guarantee (risk reversal)
- Price reveal (after full value established)
- Genuine urgency only (event-only pricing, limited seats — never manufactured scarcity)
- One clear CTA

## 5 Critical Design Principles

| Principle | What It Means |
|-----------|---------------|
| **One Distinction Rule** | The entire talk serves ONE reframe. Every story/framework/example points back to it. If you can't state it in one sentence, the talk is too scattered. |
| **The Confession Principle** | Open with vulnerability, not credentials. They trust you BECAUSE you struggled. |
| **Framework as Proof** | When you introduce proprietary work, you demonstrate deeper thinking than anyone else — authority without claiming it. |
| **The Invisible Close** | Positioning version: audience WANTS the next step without being pushed. Close version: offer feels like the logical conclusion of everything learned. |
| **Audience Energy Management** | 3–4 energy cycles per 30 min. Never one flat energy level for more than 7 minutes. |

## 7 Output Sections

When running this prompt, generate all 7 parts:

1. **Talk Overview** — 3 title options (with reasoning), recommended title, tagline, The One Sentence, paradigm shift (FROM/TO), audience state (walking in / walking out)
2. **Talk Architecture** — Full 4-Act breakdown with opening lines, beats, transitions, and energy notes
3. **Delivery Notes** — Energy map table (minute-by-minute), pacing rules, common mistakes
4. **Adaptation Guide** — How to reshape for podcast, webinar, conference breakout (20 min), workshop (half-day)
5. **Q&A Playbook** — 10 anticipated questions with why-they-ask-it and response framework
6. **Video Extraction Map** — 6 clip opportunities with timestamps, duration, platform, and caption hooks
7. **Architecture Checklist** — Pre-rehearsal verification across Structure, Content, Positioning, and Delivery

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Starting with credentials ("20 years in...") | Start with failure instead |
| Teaching multiple frameworks | One distinction, one framework, taught deeply |
| Apologizing for the close module | If you're selling, own it |
| Running over time | End early. Respect their schedule. |
| Skipping the diagnostic | Self-discovery is more powerful than telling |
| "Does anyone have questions?" close | Always close with your One Sentence |

## Quick Reference: Talk Versions by Venue

| Venue | Time | Key Adjustments |
|-------|------|-----------------|
| Conference breakout | 20 min | Act 1 = 2 min, ONE framework component, no diagnostic, lead magnet CTA only |
| Podcast guest | 30–60 min | Skip Act 1 setup (host introduces you), lead with Act 2 reframe, framework as conversation |
| Webinar | 45–60 min | Expand Act 3, add second diagnostic, lean into close module |
| Workshop / Half-day | 2–4 hours | Diagnostic = full 30–45 min exercise, group work per component, implementation time |
