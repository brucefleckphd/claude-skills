# Belief Gap Analyzer

Maps the exact path from where prospects ARE (Point A beliefs) to where they need to BE (Point B beliefs) to make purchase inevitable. Shows not just WHAT beliefs to change, but in what ORDER and using what EVIDENCE.

---

## LAYER 1: INVARIANTS

1. **Order matters absolutely.** Some beliefs cannot be installed until prerequisite beliefs exist. Attempting to build Belief 4 before Belief 1 guarantees failure. Every sequence must respect dependency logic.
2. **Evidence type is not interchangeable.** Logical evidence doesn't fix emotional resistance. Story doesn't fix category skepticism. Match evidence type to gap type or the bridge fails.
3. **The Core Concept, if produced, is the Master Bridge.** Identify it explicitly — it's the single belief that, once established, shortens or eliminates the need for multiple other bridges.
4. **Point B must be purchase-ready.** If someone at Point B wouldn't buy, you've defined Point B incorrectly. Revise before mapping.
5. **Save all outputs to files.** Never deliver in chat only.

---

## PURPOSE

The formula: **Desire + Belief = Demand**

Desire exists at Level 1. But desire alone doesn't produce demand. Between desire and purchase is a specific belief structure. This skill maps that structure.

The Bridge Metaphor: Point A and Point B are two cliffs separated by a chasm. You can't jump it in one leap. You build bridges — one belief at a time — that create a safe, logical path from where prospects are to where they need to be.

**Critical Insight:** The ORDER matters. Some bridges enable others:
- Can't believe "your solution works" until they believe "solutions in this category can work"
- Can't believe "I can do this" until they believe "it's not my fault I failed before"
- Can't believe "now is the right time" until they believe "waiting costs more than acting"

**Output use:** Becomes the content strategy blueprint. Every piece of marketing content builds a specific bridge. This tells you which content to create first, why, and what proof it needs.

---

## THE THREE TYPES OF BELIEFS

**1. CAUSE BELIEFS** (Why things happen)
- Example: "I failed because I used the wrong method" (not "because I'm incompetent")
- Importance: Must correct attribution BEFORE building confidence
- Hardest to change because they're deeply rooted in experience

**2. MEANING BELIEFS** (What things mean)
- Example: "Complexity means thorough" vs. "Complexity means over-complicated"
- Importance: Same fact, different interpretation, different decision
- Often shifted by reframing (the Core Concept works here)

**3. IDENTITY BELIEFS** (Who I am)
- Example: "I'm someone who succeeds with this" vs. "this isn't for someone like me"
- Importance: Most powerful and hardest to change
- Must be addressed or purchase becomes identity-threatening

## STEP 2: DEPENDENCY MAPPING

For each belief bridge, map:
- What beliefs must exist BEFORE this one?
- What beliefs does this one ENABLE?
- Is this a Foundation, Middle, Upper, or Capstone bridge?

**Foundation Bridges** (must come first, enable others):
- No prerequisites
- Establish the emotional/logical safety for everything that follows
- Examples: "It's not your fault" / "This category of solution can work" / "You're not the problem, your method is"

**Middle Bridges** (depend on foundation, enable upper):
- Prerequisites: specific foundation bridges
- Build specific confidence and mechanism understanding

**Upper Bridges** (advanced, require multiple prerequisites):
- Build product-specific confidence and solution fit
- Address "will this work FOR ME?"

**Capstone Bridges** (purchase readiness):
- Investment justification
- Timing urgency
- Final risk removal

---

## STEP 3: OBSTACLE ANALYSIS

For each bridge, identify:
- What limiting beliefs actively interfere?
- What fears must be addressed before this bridge is possible?
- What past failures created resistance?
- What proof is needed to overcome skepticism?

---

## STEP 4: EVIDENCE SPECIFICATION

For each bridge, specify:
- **Evidence type:** Story / Data / Demonstration / Logic / Authority
- **Specific proof:** What exactly would convince them
- **Format:** Testimonial / Case study / Statistic / Explanation / Demo
- **Mistakes to avoid:** What evidence types won't work here and why

**Evidence type matching rules:**
- Identity gaps → Story (not logic — they need to see themselves in someone else's journey)
- Cause gaps → Logic + story (reframe the attribution, then prove it with example)
- Category trust gaps → Acknowledge + differentiate (don't defend the category, reframe your position within it)
- Self-efficacy gaps → Social proof from someone similar (not aspirational — peer-level)
- Timing gaps → Quantified cost of waiting (make delay expensive)

---

## STEP 5: SEQUENCING OPTIMIZATION

Arrange all bridges in the optimal order considering:
- Logical dependencies (A must precede B)
- Psychological readiness (can't accept X until feeling Y)
- Momentum building (early wins enable later challenges)
- Natural narrative flow (tells a coherent story)

Produce:
```
BRIDGE 1: [Name] — [Type]
    ↓ enables
BRIDGE 2: [Name] — [Type]
    ↓ enables
BRIDGE 3: [Name] — [Type]
    [continues...]
    ↓ ready for
PURCHASE CONVERSATION
```

## TROUBLESHOOTING GUIDE

**"Prospects aren't crossing Bridge #X"**
- Is prerequisite bridge solid? (Test it separately)
- Is proof type wrong? (Logic when emotion needed, or vice versa)
- Is the bridge too big? (Break into 2-3 smaller bridges)

**"Prospects cross bridges but still don't buy"**
- Is Point B definition complete? (Review — you may have defined an incomplete Point B)
- Is there a hidden gap you didn't identify? (Search for identity beliefs)
- Are bridges solid or just surface-accepted? (Test: can they explain in their own words?)

**"Sequence feels too long"**
- Can any bridges be combined?
- Can any be built in parallel?
- Is there a Master Bridge (Core Concept) that shortcuts multiple steps?
