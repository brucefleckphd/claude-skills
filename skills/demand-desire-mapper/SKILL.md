# Demand Desire Mapper

Maps the complete desire architecture from unchangeable L1 desires through the belief layers that channel them into demand.

---

## LAYER 1: INVARIANTS

1. **L1 desires are NEVER created — they are identified.** Reese's 16 desires are empirically established. You map which ones are active for this market, not which ones would be convenient.
2. **Each level of the hierarchy must logically connect to the next.** L2 channels L1. L3 channels L2. L4 produces demand. A gap at any level = a gap in the marketing.
3. **Every claim about desire channeling must be grounded in actual market evidence** — not assumptions. Use available research, quotes, reviews, testimonials as evidence.
4. **Save all outputs to files.** Never deliver analysis only in chat.

---

## PURPOSE

Answers the fundamental question that precedes all marketing work:

> "Which of the 16 unchangeable human desires does my product channel — and through what belief architecture does it do so?"

Schwartz knew desires channel into demand but couldn't map the mechanism. Reese identified the 16 L1 desires scientifically but didn't connect them to demand creation. D3 maps the exact belief layers between them. This skill synthesizes all three.

**Output use:** The completed hierarchy becomes the master brief for all downstream work — psychographic research, avatar development, USP generation, Core Concept development, and messaging.

## FRAMEWORK: THE THREE SYNTHESES

### Synthesis 1: Reese's 16 Basic Desires (Level 1)

Scientific L1 desires — each person has all 16, but at different intensities (bell curve). These DEFINE personality and are UNCHANGEABLE:

| Desire | Core Drive |
|--------|-----------|
| Power | Influence, achievement, leadership |
| Independence | Freedom, autonomy, self-reliance |
| Curiosity | Learning, truth-seeking, mental stimulation |
| Acceptance | Belonging, approval, social connection |
| Order | Organization, certainty, predictability |
| Saving | Wealth preservation, frugality, accumulation |
| Honor | Integrity, loyalty, moral code |
| Idealism | Justice, equality, cause-driven meaning |
| Social Contact | Companionship, friendship, camaraderie |
| Family | Parenting, legacy, generational thinking |
| Status | Prestige, reputation, recognition |
| Vengeance | Competition, victory, getting what's deserved |
| Romance | Beauty, partnership, attraction |
| Eating | Pleasure, comfort, sensory experience |
| Physical Activity | Health, strength, vitality |
| Tranquility | Safety, peace, freedom from anxiety |

### Synthesis 2: Schwartz's Channeling Insight (the bridge)

Desires don't create demand on their own. Between L1 and purchase is a **channeling process** — a specific belief structure that directs desire toward a particular solution. Schwartz identified this happens; D3 maps how.

### Synthesis 3: D3 Belief Architecture (Levels 2-4)

- **L2: Category Beliefs** — beliefs about the type of solution that can satisfy the L1 desire ("AI tools can help me achieve independence")
- **L3: Product Beliefs** — beliefs about whether THIS specific product can deliver the category promise ("Rich's methodology specifically works for agency owners like me")
- **L4: Self-Efficacy Beliefs** — beliefs about whether THEY can successfully implement ("I can execute this despite my past failures")

**The D3 formula: Desire + Belief = Demand**

When all four levels align, purchase becomes inevitable. When any level has a gap, marketing fails.

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

### Phase 1: L1 Desire Identification

Analyze the market and identify:

1. **Primary L1 desires** (top 2-3 that drive this market most powerfully)
2. **Secondary L1 desires** (supporting desires that amplify the primary)
3. **Suppressed desires** (desires the market wants but won't admit publicly — often most powerful)
4. **Evidence for each** — specific quotes, behaviors, or patterns showing these desires are active

For each primary desire, answer:
- How does this desire manifest in THIS specific market?
- What does a person with high intensity of this desire look like in this context?
- What would they sacrifice to have this desire satisfied?

### Phase 2: L2 Category Belief Mapping

For each primary L1 desire:

1. **What category of solution does this market believe can satisfy it?**
2. **What limiting L2 beliefs exist?** ("That category of solution doesn't work for people like me")
3. **What enabling L2 beliefs must be created?**
4. **What is the current market sophistication level for this category?** (Schwartz levels 1-5)

### Phase 3: L3 Product Belief Mapping

1. **What specific product beliefs are required for purchase?**
   - Belief about mechanism: "This methodology actually works because..."
   - Belief about differentiation: "This is different from what I've tried because..."
   - Belief about fit: "This was designed for someone in my situation because..."

2. **What L3 beliefs are blocking purchase right now?**
   - Past failures in the category
   - Skepticism about the provider's ability to deliver
   - Doubt about uniqueness vs. competitors

### Phase 4: L4 Self-Efficacy Belief Mapping

1. **What do they believe about THEIR ability to succeed?**
2. **What past failure patterns undermine self-efficacy?**
3. **What must change in how they see themselves for purchase to feel rational?**

### Phase 5: The Channel Map

Produce a visual representation showing:
```
L1 PRIMARY DESIRE: [Name and description]
        ↓ channels through
L2 CATEGORY BELIEF: "[What category of solution addresses this]"
        ↓ channels through
L3 PRODUCT BELIEF: "[Why this specific product fits]"
        ↓ channels through
L4 SELF-BELIEF: "[Why they can succeed with this]"
        ↓ produces
DEMAND: [Purchase decision becomes inevitable when all 4 align]
```

Produce this map for each primary L1 desire identified.

### Phase 6: Gap Analysis

For each channel:
- **Where is the channel STRONG?** (Existing beliefs that already support purchase)
- **Where is the channel BROKEN?** (Missing or blocking beliefs that interrupt demand)
- **What is the highest-leverage intervention point?** (The single belief that, if created, would unblock the most demand)
