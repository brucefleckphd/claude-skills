# Loewenstein Visceral Audit

---

## LAYER 1: INVARIANTS (Never override.)

1. **Name specific visceral factors, not "emotions."** Fear, excitement, urgency, FOMO, status anxiety, social pressure, competitive arousal, and relief are DIFFERENT visceral factors with different intensity curves, different half-lives, and different behavioral consequences. The audit must identify WHICH specific factors are being triggered at each point, not label everything as "emotional selling."

2. **Map intensity on a continuum.** Each visceral factor operates from 0 (absent) to 10 (fully commandeering behavior). At low levels (1-3), the factor mildly influences preference. At moderate levels (4-6), it meaningfully shapes decision-making. At high levels (7-10), it effectively overrides rational evaluation. The skill must score intensity AND assess whether each CTA falls within the appropriate intensity range.

3. **The window between visceral peak and CTA determines conversion.** If the CTA (buy button, signup, call booking) is placed at the moment of maximum visceral intensity, conversion is highest. If the CTA is placed before the peak, the buyer isn't ready. If it's placed after the peak, the visceral state has already begun fading and rational evaluation is reasserting. Map the timing gap between peak and CTA for every identified sequence.

4. **Visceral factors have short half-lives.** Unlike beliefs or attitudes (which are relatively stable), visceral states change quickly. Fear generated by a scarcity email at 9am has largely dissipated by noon. This means: the farther the buyer is from the visceral trigger when they encounter the CTA, the less effective the trigger is. Multi-step funnels (email → landing page → checkout) lose visceral intensity at each step unless they RE-TRIGGER.

5. **Ethical calibration is mandatory.** The skill must assess whether visceral intensity at the point of purchase is APPROPRIATE — strong enough to motivate action, not so strong that the buyer cannot evaluate the offer. Flag sequences where visceral factors are deliberately maximized to a level that impairs rational evaluation, especially when combined with: high price, non-refundable terms, irreversible commitment, or vulnerable populations.

---

## LAYER 2: QUALITY GUIDANCE

6. **Compound visceral states are exponentially more powerful.** Fear + urgency > fear alone. Excitement + social proof > excitement alone. When multiple visceral factors fire simultaneously, the combined effect is greater than the sum. Identify compound visceral states and assess whether they're deliberate or accidental.

7. **Visceral factors can work AGAINST conversion.** Fear of making a bad decision, anxiety about being judged, dread of implementation complexity — these are visceral factors that PREVENT purchase. They're not "objections" (cognitive) — they're feelings. The audit must map both pro-purchase and anti-purchase visceral factors.

8. **Time-of-day effects are real.** Decision fatigue, hunger, tiredness, and post-lunch cognitive dips affect visceral vulnerability. If the marketing sequence involves scheduled events (webinars, sales calls), assess whether the timing interacts with predictable visceral states.

9. **The wanting/liking distinction applies here.** Visceral factors primarily drive WANTING (acquisition motivation). They do not predict LIKING (satisfaction during consumption). High-visceral-intensity sales followed by low-visceral-intensity delivery creates the wanting/liking gap. Flag this when detected.

## PURPOSE

Every effective funnel triggers visceral responses — that's not manipulation, that's how human motivation works. But most marketers don't know WHICH visceral factors they're triggering, whether those factors are calibrated, whether the timing aligns with action points, or whether they're creating visceral states that will produce buyer's remorse.

This skill audits the complete visceral landscape of a marketing sequence. It names every factor, scores its intensity, maps its timing against CTAs, evaluates calibration, identifies compound states, and flags ethical concerns.

## EXECUTION PROCESS

### Step 1: Visceral Factor Inventory

Walk through every element of the marketing sequence. At each point, identify:
- **Which visceral factors are being triggered?** (fear, excitement, urgency, FOMO, competitive arousal, social pressure, relief, curiosity-as-itch, status anxiety, hope, anger at current situation)
- **How are they being triggered?** (specific copy, design, timing, social elements)
- **What intensity?** (1-10 scale)
- **What is the half-life?** (how quickly does this state fade?)

### Step 2: Intensity Curve Mapping

Plot visceral intensity across the full sequence. Mark:
- **Peaks** — moments of maximum visceral intensity
- **Valleys** — moments where visceral state is neutral/calm
- **Sustained plateaus** — stretches where intensity holds steady
- **Compound zones** — where multiple factors fire simultaneously

### Step 3: CTA Timing Analysis

For every call to action in the sequence, measure:
- **Distance from nearest visceral peak** — is the CTA at the peak, or displaced?
- **Dominant visceral factor at CTA** — which factor is strongest when the buyer hits the button?
- **Intensity at CTA** — is it in the action zone (4-7) or the impairment zone (8-10)?
- **Anti-purchase factors at CTA** — which visceral factors are working AGAINST action at this point?

### Step 4: Re-Trigger Assessment

For multi-step sequences (email → page → checkout), assess:
- Does each step RE-TRIGGER visceral factors, or does it rely on carry-over from the previous step?
- What intensity is lost at each transition? (clicking from email to page typically loses 30-50% of visceral intensity)
- Are there "cold spots" where the buyer transitions between heated environments?

### Step 5: Calibration Assessment

For the purchase moment specifically, evaluate:
- **Total visceral intensity** — sum of all pro-purchase factors minus anti-purchase factors
- **Rational evaluation capacity** — can the buyer still think clearly at this intensity level?
- **Proportionality** — is the visceral intensity proportional to the offer? (high intensity for a $47 product is suspicion-inducing; low intensity for a $5,000 product leaves money on the table)
- **Post-purchase prediction** — based on the visceral state at purchase, predict the buyer's emotional state 48 hours later

### Step 6: Ethical Evaluation

Flag any of these patterns:
- Visceral intensity above 8 combined with non-refundable terms
- Deliberate exploitation of fear without proportional hope
- Time pressure that prevents any rational evaluation
- Compound visceral states targeting known vulnerability factors
- Re-trigger patterns designed to prevent the buyer from ever entering a calm evaluation state

### Step 7: Recommendations

Produce specific recommendations:
- Where to ADD visceral triggers (dead zones where the buyer has no emotional momentum)
- Where to REDUCE visceral triggers (impairment zones where intensity is counterproductive)
- Where to RETIMING CTAs (misalignment between peaks and action points)
- Where to ADD re-triggers (multi-step transitions losing too much intensity)
- How to BALANCE pro-purchase and anti-purchase factors
- How to CALIBRATE for the specific price point and audience

### Step 8: Self-Examination

Surface the client's visceral-factor blind spots:
- Are they creating visceral intensity they can't sustain through delivery? (high-intensity sale → low-intensity onboarding)
- Are they confusing "urgent" with "important"?
- Are they triggering fear when they should be triggering excitement?
- Are they accidentally creating anti-purchase visceral states (complexity anxiety, price dread)?
