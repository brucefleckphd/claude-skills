# Deutsch Critic - Voice Patterns Reference

This document provides quick reference to Deutsch's voice patterns for use during copy evaluation.

---

## THE JUST TALK VOICE

### What It Sounds Like
- Conversational, like explaining across a table
- Natural speech patterns
- Your inner persuader, not your inner writer

### Conversational Markers (Should Have)
- "Here's the thing..."
- "I'm going to let you in on something..."
- "Now, I know what you're thinking..."
- "Look..."
- "Let me tell you what this really means..."
- "The truth is..."
- "And here's why that matters..."

### Red Flags (Should NOT Have)
- Overly formal language
- "Marketing speak"
- Corporate memo tone
- Textbook language
- "Writer brain" - too polished, too clever
- Anything triggering "I'm being sold to"

## THE 10 EMOTIONAL TRIGGER WORD BANKS

### 1. Fear Words
- Danger, threat, risk, warning, beware
- Crisis, collapse, disaster, catastrophe
- Lose, losing, lost, gone forever
- Before it's too late, running out of time
- Vulnerable, exposed, unprotected

### 2. Gain Words (What Money BRINGS)
- Freedom, independence, control
- Wealth, fortune, prosperity
- Costa Rica beach, Disneyland with grandkids
- Dancing at weddings, picking up grandkids
- Taking care of family

### 3. Ease Words
- Simple, easy, effortless
- No work required, automatic
- Done for you, hands-off
- In just minutes, while you sleep

### 4. Edge/Advantage Words
- Secret, hidden, insider
- Advantage, edge, upper hand
- What they don't want you to know
- Private, exclusive, members only

### 5. Absolution Words
- It's not your fault
- They lied to you, you were misled
- Nobody told you, they kept it hidden
- The real culprit is...

### 6. Insider Status Words
- Inner circle, elite group
- Founding member, charter member
- Invitation only, by application only
- Fellow [insider], like-minded

### 7. Freedom Words
- Free from, independent of
- No more, never again
- Break free, escape
- On your own terms, your way

### 8. Recognition Words
- You deserve, you've earned
- Finally get credit, be acknowledged
- Others will notice, they'll ask you
- Featured, highlighted, showcased

### 9. Payback/Revenge Words
- Show them, prove them wrong
- Get the last laugh, sweet revenge
- Vindication, justice, payback
- Who's laughing now

### 10. Transformation Words
- Transform, become, evolve
- New you, different person
- Reinvent, start over, fresh start
- Life will never be the same

## THE DUAL JOURNEY VOICE

### Intellectual Journey Markers
- "Here's why this matters..."
- "The reason is..."
- "Research shows..."
- "Because of this... therefore..."
- "First... Then... Therefore..."
- Clear causation language

### Emotional Journey Markers
- "Imagine what it would feel like..."
- "You know that feeling when..."
- "Picture yourself..."
- "That moment when..."
- Escalating emotional stakes
- Future pacing language

---

## HEADLINE VOICE BY MARKET STAGE

### Stage 1-2 (Direct)
- "Lose weight"
- "Make money investing"
- Straight benefit statement

### Stage 3-4 (Mechanism)
- "Lose weight while you sleep"
- "Thanks to this little-known..."
- HOW you do it is the hook

### Stage 5 (Indirect/Curiosity)
- "Why French women never get fat"
- "If you've tried everything and failed..."
- New perspective, not claim

---

## CLOSE VOICE

### Future Pace Voice
- "Imagine waking up and..."
- "Picture yourself..."
- "What would it feel like..."
- Vivid, sensory, specific

### Crossroads Close Voice
- "You're at a crossroads right now"
- "You have two choices"
- "Path 1: Keep doing what you're doing..."
- "Path 2: Take action today..."
- "This isn't about buying - it's about CHANGING YOUR LIFE"

### Guarantee Voice (POSITIVE, not negative)
- WRONG: "If you hate it, we'll refund you"
- RIGHT: "You've got to love it, or..."
- "The reason I guarantee this is because I KNOW it works"

### Passion Close Voice
- Life-changing energy
- Not "please click the button"
- "This is a life-changing moment for you"

## STORY VOICE

### Story Energy Markers
- People doing stuff
- Action and conflict
- Struggle and fighting
- Explosions, disasters, body on floor
- Grandmother stories

### Story Beginnings
- "I'll never forget the day..."
- "She didn't know it yet, but..."
- "That's when everything changed..."
- "Against all odds..."

### Story Tension
- "But then..."
- "That's when everything changed..."
- "Little did he know..."
- "The worst was yet to come..."

---

## SIMPLICITY MARKERS

### Caveman Simple Language
- Short sentences for emphasis
- Everyday vocabulary
- Concrete pictures, not abstractions
- One idea per paragraph

### The Trump Test
"Build a wall. Make Mexico pay for it."
- Everybody understands
- Smart, not smart, rich, poor
- Caveman simple wins

---

## VOICE QUALITY CHECKLIST

When evaluating, check:

- [ ] Conversational tone (Just Talk)
- [ ] No marketing speak
- [ ] Would pass Dear Mom test
- [ ] Reads aloud naturally
- [ ] Multiple emotional triggers present
- [ ] Dual journey markers visible
- [ ] Caveman simple throughout
- [ ] Future pace vivid and specific
- [ ] Crossroads decision, not purchase
- [ ] Passion in the close
